Let \(F\) be the splitting field over \(\Q\) of the polynomial \(f(x)=x^3-2\text{,}\) the roots of which are:
  1. \(\displaystyle \sqrt[3]2\)
  2. \(\sqrt[3]2e^{2\pi i/3}\text{,}\) and
  3. \(\sqrt[3]2e^{4\pi i/3}\text{.}\) Using Eisenstein's Criterion with \(p=2\) we see that \(f\) is irreducible in \(\Q[x]\text{.}\) As \(f\) is monic and irreducible it is the minimum polynomial of \(\sqrt[3]2\in\Q[x]\) and \([\Q(\sqrt[3]2):\Q]=3\text{.}\) Thus, by the FTGT we know there exists an element of order \(3\) in \(\Gal(F:\Q)\text{.}\)
