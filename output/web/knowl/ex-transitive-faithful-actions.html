Example 4.8. Transitive and Faithful Actions.

  1. The action of the group of rotational symmetries of a regular polygon on the vertices of the polygon is transitive. Any vertex can be sent to any other vertex by a rotation of the polygon.
  2. The action of the group of rigid motions of three-dimensional space on the points of a sphere is transitive. Any point on the sphere can be sent to any other point by a rotation or translation of space.
  3. The action of the group of invertible \(2\times2\) matrices on the complex plane by multiplication is faithful. Every non-identity matrix corresponds to a different transformation of the complex plane.
  4. The action of the group of permutations of a set \(X\) on the set \(X\) itself by permutation is faithful. Every permutation corresponds to a unique rearrangement of the elements of \(X\text{.}\)
  5. The action of the group of permutations of a set \(X\) on the set of all subsets of \(X\) by permutation is both transitive and faithful. Any subset can be sent to any other subset by permuting the elements of \(X\text{,}\) and every non-identity permutation corresponds to a different permutation of the subsets of \(X\text{.}\)
