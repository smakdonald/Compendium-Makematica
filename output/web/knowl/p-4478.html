Notice that \(E=\Q(\sqrt{ 2 },\sqrt{ 5 })\) which also has degree \(4\text{.}\) Let \(G\) denote the Galois group of \(E/\Q\text{.}\) Thus \(G\) is a group of order \(4\text{,}\) making it isomorphic to \(\Z_2\times\Z_2\) or \(\Z_4\text{.}\) Notice that the elements of \(G\) are the following: - \(\id(\sqrt{ 2 })=\sqrt{ 2 }\) and \(\id(\sqrt{ 5 })=\sqrt{ 5 }\text{,}\) - \(\tau(\sqrt{ 2 })=\sqrt{ 2 }\) and \(\s(\sqrt{ 5 })=-\sqrt{ 5 }\text{,}\) - \(\g(\sqrt{ 2 })=-\sqrt{ 2 }\) and \(\s(\sqrt{ 5 })=-\sqrt{ 5 }\text{,}\) and - \(\l(\sqrt{ 2 })=-\sqrt{ 2 }\) and \(\s(\sqrt{ 5 })=\sqrt{ 5 }\text{.}\) All of these automorphisms have degree \(2\text{,}\) making \(G\cong\Z_2\times\Z_2\text{.}\)
  3. Thus are only two subgroups of \(G\text{,}\) \(H\cong\Z_2\) and \(K\cong\Z_2\text{.}\) The first, \(H\text{,}\) corresponds to \(\Q(\sqrt{ 2 })\) and is generated by \(\sqrt{ 2 }\text{,}\) where \(K\) corresponds to \(\Q(\sqrt{ 5 })\) and is generated by \(\sqrt{ 5 }\text{.}\)
