Theorem 5.27. Fundamental Theorem of Finitely Generated Modules over a PID, Invariant Factors Form.

Let \(R\) be a PID and let \(M\) be a finitely generated module. Then there exist integers \(r \geq 0, k \geq 0\text{,}\) and non-zero, non-unit elements \(d_1,\ldots, d_k\) of \(R\) satisfying \(d_1 \mid d_2 \mid \dots \mid d_k\) such that
\begin{equation*} M \cong R^r \oplus R/(d_1) \oplus \dots \oplus R/(d_k). \end{equation*}
Moreover \(r\) and \(k\) are uniquely determined by \(M\text{,}\) and the \(d_i\)'s are unique up to associates.
