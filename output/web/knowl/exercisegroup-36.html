3.
Let \(G\) be a (not necessarily finite) group and \(K \leq G\) a subgroup of index \(n < \infty\text{.}\) Define
\begin{equation*} N=\bigcap_{g\in G}gKg\inv \end{equation*}
(i.e., \(N\) is the intersection of all the conjugates of K\()\)
  1. Prove \(N\) is a the largest normal subgroup of \(G\) that is contained in \(K\text{.}\)
  2. Prove \([G : N ]\) divides \(n!\text{.}\)

4.
Let \(H\) be a subgroup of a group \(G\text{.}\) Recall that the centralizer of \(H\) is the subgroup of \(G\) defined by \(C_G(H) = \{g \in G | gh = hg ∀h \in H\}\) and the normalizer of \(H\) is the subgroup of \(G\) defined by \(N_G(H) = \{g \in G | gHg\inv = H\}\text{.}\)
  1. Show that the centralizer \(C_G(H)\) of \(H\) in \(G\) is a normal subgroup of the normalizer \(N_G(H)\) of \(H\) in \(G\text{.}\)
  2. Show that the quotient \(N_G(H)/C_G(H)\) is isomorphic to a subgroup of the automorphism group \(\Aut(H)\) of \(H\text{.}\)
