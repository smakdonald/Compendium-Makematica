Summary 1.1.

  • A Group is a set equipped with a Binary Operation that satisfies the properties given in Definition 1.2. An abelian group is a group that also satisfies the Commutative Property.
  • The identity and inverse elements described in Definition 1.2 are unique.  8  For inverses, we have \((x^{-1})^{-1} = x\) and \((xy)\inv=y\inv x\inv\text{.}\)  9 
  • \((\Z,+), (\Q,+), (\R,+),(\C,+)\) and \((\Z/n,+)\) are all additive abelian groups, and Groups of Units form multiplicative abelian groups.
