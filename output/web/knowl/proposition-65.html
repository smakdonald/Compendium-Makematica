Proposition 14.25.

Recall that a \(\Z\)-module \(M\) is called torsion-free if the only element \(m \in M\) such that \(r \cdot m = 0\)for some non-zero integer \(r\) is the element \(m = 0\text{.}\) Let \(M = \coker(T_A)\) where \(T_A: \Z \to \Z^2\) is the homomorphism \(T_A(v) = Av\) for the matrix \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 11 \\ \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) Prove \(M\) is torsion-free.
