Item D.1.4.b.
Let \(R\) be a finite ring, and let \(P\) be a prime ideal in \(R\text{.}\) By Theorem 9.31 \(R/P\) is a finite integral domain. We show \(R/P\) is a field. Let \(a\not\in P\text{,}\) and consider \(a^n\) for all \(n\in\N\text{.}\) As \(R/P\) is finite, there exists some \(k<m\in N\) such that \(a^k=a^m\text{.}\) Then \(a^{m-k}\cdot a^k=a^m=a^k\text{,}\) meaning that \(a^{m-k}=e\text{.}\) Thus \(a\) has inverse \(a^{m-k-1}\text{,}\) making \(a\) a unit. Thus \(R/P\) is a field, making \(P\) a maximal ideal by Theorem 9.36.
