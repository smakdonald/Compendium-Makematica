Let \(H\) and \(K\) be groups. Recall that for any group \(G\text{,}\) an automorphism of \(G\) is an isomorphism from \(G\) to \(G\text{,}\) and \(\Aut(G)\) denotes the group of automorphisms of \(G\text{.}\)
  1. Show that the direct product group \(\Aut(H)\times\Aut(K)\) is isomorphic to a subgroup of \(\Aut(H\times K)\text{.}\)
  2. Give an example, with justification, of groups \(H\) and \(K\) for which \(\Aut(H)\times\Aut(K)\) is not isomorphic to \(\Aut(H\times K)\text{.}\)
