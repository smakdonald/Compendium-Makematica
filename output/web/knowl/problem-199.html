Problem G.167. Problem 4.

Fix a prime number \(p\text{,}\) and let \(A\) denote the abelian group of all complex roots of unity whose orders are powers of \(p\text{;}\) that is
\begin{equation*} A = \{z \in \C | z^{p^n} = 1 \text{ for some integer } n \leq 1\} . \end{equation*}
Prove the following statements. (a) Every non-trivial subgroup of \(A\) contains the group of \(p^{\text{th}}\) roots of unity. (b) Every proper subgroup of \(A\) is cyclic. (c) If \(B\) and \(C\) are subgroups of \(A\text{,}\) then either \(B \subseteq C\) or \(C \subseteq B\text{.}\) (d) For each \(n \geq 0\) there exists a unique subgroup of \(A\) with \(p^n\) elements.
