3. May 2021 Problem (1).
Let \(G\) be a group (not necessarily finite) and \(H\) a nonempty subset of \(G\) that is closed under multiplication. Suppose that for all \(g\in G\) we have \(g^2\in H\text{.}\) Prove the following:
  1. \(H\) is a subgroup of \(G\)
  2. \(H\) is normal
  3. \(G/H\) is abelian.

4. May 2019 Problem 2.
Let \(H\) be a subgroup of a group \(G\text{.}\) Recall that the centralizer of \(H\) is the subgroup of \(G\) defined by \(Z_G(H) = \{g \in G | gh = hg ∀h \in H\}\)  2  and the normalizer of \(H\) is the subgroup of \(G\) defined by \(N_G(H) = \{g \in G | gHg\inv = H\}\text{.}\)
  1. Show that the centralizer \(C_G(H)\) of \(H\) in \(G\) is a normal subgroup of the normalizer \(N_G(H)\) of \(H\) in \(G\text{.}\)
  2. Show that the quotient \(N_G(H)/C_G(H)\) is isomorphic to a subgroup of the automorphism group \(\Aut(H)\) of \(H\text{.}\)
Solution.
5. May 2018 Problem 1.
Let \(H\) and \(K\) be groups. Recall that for any group \(G\text{,}\) an automorphism of \(G\) is an isomorphism from \(G\) to \(G\text{,}\) and \(\Aut(G)\) denotes the group of automorphisms of \(G\text{.}\)
  1. Show that the direct product group \(\Aut(H)\times\Aut(K)\) is isomorphic to a subgroup of \(\Aut(H\times K)\text{.}\)
  2. Give an example, with justification, of groups \(H\) and \(K\) for which \(\Aut(H)\times\Aut(K)\) is not isomorphic to \(\Aut(H\times K)\text{.}\)
