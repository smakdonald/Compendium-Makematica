Item 4.

Let \(K\) be a field, or more generally a division ring, and let \(R=M_n(K) \cong \operatorname{End}_K(K^n)\text{.}\) The module \(M=K^n\) of column vectors is a simple \(R\)-module Indeed, if \(v=(a_1,\dots,a_n)\neq 0\text{,}\) say \(a_i\neq 0\text{;}\) then \(a_i^{-1} E_{ij} v = e_i \in M\text{,}\) and since \(M\) is generated by the standard vectors \(e_i\text{,}\) \(M = Rv\text{.}\)
