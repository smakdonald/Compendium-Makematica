Let \(G\) be a group (not necessarily finite) and \(H\) a nonempty subset of \(G\) that is closed under multiplication. Suppose that for all \(g\in G\) we have \(g^2\in H\text{.}\) Prove the following: (a) \(H\) is a subgroup of \(G\) (b) \(H\) is normal (c) \(G/H\) is abelian.
