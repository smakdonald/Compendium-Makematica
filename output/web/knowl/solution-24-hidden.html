Let \(G\) be a group and \(H\nsg G\text{.}\) Let \(x\in C_H(G)\text{,}\) and consider \(gxg\inv\text{.}\) Let \(h\in H\text{.}\) As \(H\nsg G\) we have \(h=gh'g\inv\) for some \(h\in H\text{,}\) and thus that
  1. \(g\inv h=h'g\inv\) and
  2. \(hg=gh'\text{.}\)
Consider \(gxg\inv h\text{.}\) By (1), we see \(gxg\inv h=gxh'g\inv\text{.}\) As \(x\) commutes with everything in \(H\) we have \(gh'xg\inv\text{,}\) and by (2) we have \(hgxg\inv\text{.}\) Thus \(C_H(G)\nsg G\text{.}\)
Let \(G\) act on the left cosets of \(C_G(H)\) by left multiplication, giving rise to the permutation representation homomorphism \(\rho:G/C_G(H)\to\Aut(H)\text{.}\) By the First Isomorphism Theorem we see that \(G/C_G(H)\) is isomorphic to a subgroup of the automorphism group of \(H\text{.}\)
