Problem F.70. Problem 7.

On canonical forms (a) Consider the \(\Q[x]\)-module
\begin{equation*} M=\frac{\Q[x]}{(x^4-1)}\oplus\frac{\Q[x]}{x^2(x-1)} \end{equation*}
and let \(V\) the \(\Q\)-vector space obtained from \(M\) by restriction of scalars along the evident inclusion \(\Q ⊆ \Q[x]\) and let \(t : V → V\) be the \(\Q\)-linear transformation given as multiplication by \(x\text{.}\) Find, with justification, the rational canonical form of \(t\text{.}\) (b) Consider the \(\C[x]\)-module
\begin{equation*} N=\frac{\C[x]}{(x^4-1)}\oplus\frac{\Q[x]}{x^2(x-1)} \end{equation*}
and let \(W\) the \(\C\)-vector space obtained from \(N\) by restriction of scalars along \(\C ⊆ \C[x]\) and let \(t : W → W\) be the \(\C\)-linear transformation given as multiplication by \(x\text{.}\) Find, with justification, the Jordan canonical form of \(t\text{.}\)
