Many texts use the notation \(C_G(g)\) for the centralzier and \([g]_c\) for conjugacy classes. However, given that the center of a group is denoted with \(Z(G)\text{,}\) we argue that \(Z_G(g)\) makes more intuitive sense for the centralizer, freeing up \(C_G(g)\) for conjugacy classes.
