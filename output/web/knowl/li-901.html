Item 2.3.13.b.
As the identity map is the only automorphism from \(\Q\to\Q\) that fixes \(\Q\) and \([\Q:\Q]=1\text{,}\) we see that \(\Q\) is Galois over itself. As all finite extensions of fields with characteristic \(0\) are separable, we see that \(L\) is also Galois over \(\Q\text{.}\) Thus \(\Q, L,\) and \(E_4\) are the only intermediate fields \(\Q \subseteq E \subseteq L\) that are Galois over \(\Q\text{.}\)
