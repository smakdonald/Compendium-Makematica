Recall also that \(S_3\) acts on \({\mathbb{R}}^3\) by permuting the basis (corresponding to the group homomorphism \(S_3 \to \operatorname{GL}_3({\mathbb{R}})\) sending a permutation to its associated permutation matrix). The subspace \(M_3 = \{(a,b,c) \in {\mathbb{R}}^3 \mid a + b + c = 0\}\) is a subrepresenation of \({\mathbb{R}}^3\) of dimension \(2\text{.}\) We claim it is irreducible: Say \(0 \ne (a,b,c) \in M_3\text{.}\)
