We have
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} (\sigma_g\circ\sigma_{g^{-1}})(s) &=\sigma_g(\sigma_{g^{-1}}(s)) & \text{ (def of composition)}\\ &=g\cdot (g^{-1} \cdot s) & (\text{ def for } \sigma_g \text{ and } \sigma_{g^{-1}})\\ &=(gg^{-1})\cdot s & \text{ (first property of a group action)}\\ &=e_G\cdot s & \text{ (group axiom)}\\ &= s &\text{ (second property of a group action)} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
thus \(\sigma_g\circ\sigma_{g^{-1}}=\text{id}_S\) and a similar argument shows that \(\sigma_{g^{-1}}\circ\sigma_{g}=\text{id}_S\)
