Consider
\begin{equation*} V=\{e, (1 2)(3 4), (1 3)(2 4), (1 4)(2 3)\}. \end{equation*}
From Example 3.20 we have \(V \nsg S_4\text{.}\) The group \(V\) has order \(4\text{,}\) making it ableian from Checkpoint 1.23. Thus every subgroup of \(V\) is normal in \(V\) by Example 3.20.
