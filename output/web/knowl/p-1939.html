What \(\Z\)-module \(M\) is presented by
\begin{equation*} A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 3 & 9 & 5 \\ 1 & -2 & 7 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ \end{bmatrix}? \end{equation*}
Formally, \(M\) is the quotient module \(M=\Z^4/\im(T_A)\text{,}\) where \(T_A:\Z^3\to \Z^4\) is defined by \(T_A(v)=Av\text{.}\) Since \(\Z^4\) is generated by its standard basis elements \(\{e_1,e_2,e_3,e_4\}\text{,}\) it follows that \(M=\Z^4/\im(T_A)\) is generated by the cosets of the \(e_i\text{.}\) To keep the notation short, we set \(m_i=e_i+\im(T_A)\text{.}\)
