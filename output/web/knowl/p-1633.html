Say we are given an isomorphism of rings \(R \cong \prod_{i=1}^k \operatorname{Mat}_{t_i}(Q_i)\) for some division rings \(Q_1,\dots,Q_k\text{.}\) Then since \(\operatorname{Mat}_{t_i}(Q_i)\) decomposes as a direct sum of \(t_i\) copies of \(N_i:=Q_i^{t_i}\text{,}\) and \(N_i\) is a simple \(\operatorname{Mat}_{t_i}(Q_i)\)-module, hence also a simple \(R\)-module, we have a semisimple \(R\)-module decomposition of \(R\) as
\begin{equation*} M_1^{\oplus n_1} \oplus \cdots \oplus M_m^{\oplus n_m} \cong R \cong N_1^{\oplus t_1} \oplus \cdots \oplus N_k^{\oplus t_k}. \end{equation*}
By Krull-Schmidt, we must have \(m=k\text{,}\) and after a permutation, \(M_i \cong N_i = Q_i^{\oplus t_i}\) and \(n_i=t_i\) for each \(i\text{.}\)
