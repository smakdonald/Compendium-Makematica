In addition to his work in mathematics, Smith was involved in the administration of Trinity College Dublin. He served as Registrar of the University from 1867 to 1884, and in 1874 he was appointed the first dean of the newly formed Faculty of Science. He was also involved in the foundation of the Royal Irish Academy, and served as its president from 1886 to 1890. Smith was widely respected for his administrative abilities as well as his mathematical accomplishments.
