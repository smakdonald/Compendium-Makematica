Solution 1.5.1.

Let's go through each property step by step:
  • Closure:.
    To show closure, we need to demonstrate that the sum of any two natural numbers is always a natural number. Since the sum of any two positive integers is still a positive integer, closure is satisfied.
  • Associativity:.
    To prove associativity, we need to show that for any three natural numbers \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(c\text{,}\) the following holds: \((a + b) + c = a + (b + c)\text{.}\) Since addition is associative in the set of natural numbers, this property is satisfied.
  • Identity element:.
    To establish the existence of an identity element, we need to find a natural number \(e\) such that for any natural number \(n\text{,}\)\(e + n = n + e = n\text{.}\) The identity element for addition is \(0\) (zero). For any natural number \(n\text{,}\) adding zero to it does not change its value, and hence, the identity element property is satisfied.
Therefore, since the natural numbers satisfy all three properties (closure, associativity, and identity element), we can conclude that the natural numbers form a monoid under addition.
