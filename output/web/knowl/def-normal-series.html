Definition B.5. Normal Series.

For a group \(G\text{,}\) a normal series for \(G\) is a chain of subgroups
\begin{equation*} \{e\} = N_0 \leq N_1 \leq N_2 \leq \cdots \leq N_k = G \end{equation*}
for some \(k \geq 0\) such that for all \(i = 0, \cdots, k-1\) we have \(N_i \unlhd N_{i+1}\text{.}\) We write such a thing as
\begin{equation*} \{e\} = N_0 \unlhd N_1 \unlhd N_2 \unlhd\cdots \unlhd N_k = G. \end{equation*}
We call \(k\) the length of the normal series and the groups \(N_i/N_{i-1}\) for \(i = 1, \dots, k\) are called the factors of the normal series.
