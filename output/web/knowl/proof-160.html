Proof

Pick \(x \in D \smallsetminus {\mathbb{R}}\text{.}\) Then \({\mathbb{R}}\subsetneqq {\mathbb{R}}[x] \subseteq D\text{,}\) and since \({\mathbb{R}}[x]\) is an \({\mathbb{R}}\)-vectorspace, we must have \({\mathbb{R}}[x] = D\) for dimension reasons. Thus \(D\) is commutative and is a field. Since \(D\) is a finite extension of \({\mathbb{R}}\text{,}\) it is algebraic, so \({\mathbb{R}}\subsetneqq D \subseteq \mathbb{C}\text{,}\) and we must have \(D=\mathbb{C}\text{.}\)
