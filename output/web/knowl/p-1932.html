Assume \(R\) is a non-zero integral domain, but that it is not a field. Prove there exists an \(R\)-module homomorphism that is an injection but {} a split injection. {}: Pick \(a \in R\) such that \(a \neq 0\) and \(a\) is not a unit, let \(I\) be the proper ideal \(I = R \cdot a\) generated by \(a\) and show the inclusion map \(i: I \to R\) is not a split injection.
