Consider the polynomial \(x^2-3\text{.}\) This has a root \(\sqrt{3}\text{,}\) which is not in \(\Q\text{.}\) Thus, as \(x^2-3\) has degree 2, it is irreducible by Theorem 2.2 [provisional cross-reference: empty], making it the minimal polynomial of \(\Q(\sqrt{3})\text{.}\) Thus by Theorem 2.31 [provisional cross-reference: empty] we see that \([\Q(\sqrt{3}):\Q]=2\text{.}\)
