Exercise 2.
  1. \(\Q^\times=\Q\setminus\{0\}\text{,}\) \(\R^\times=\R\setminus\{0\}\text{,}\) and \(\C^\times=\C\setminus\{0\}\) are each abelian groups under multiplicaton.
  2. Let \(p\) be a prime integer. Then \(\mathbb{Z}/(p)^\times=\mathbb{Z}/(p)\setminus\{0\}\) is an abelian group under multiplicaton.
These are examples of groups of units, which will be defined explicitely and explored more generally later on See: [provisional cross-reference: def-group-of-units]
