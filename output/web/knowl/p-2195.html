Let \(B' = \{b'_1, \dots, b'_b\}\) be any other basis of \(V\text{.}\) Then \([b'_i]^B = b_i\) and so the change of basis matrix \([\id_V]_{B'}^{B}\) satisfies, on the one hand,
\begin{equation*} [\id_V]_{B'}^{B} [b'_i]^{B'}=[\id_V]_{B'}^{B} b_i = \text{the $i$-th column of $[\id_V]_{B'}^{B}$} \end{equation*}
and, on the other hand,
\begin{equation*} [\id_V]_{B'}^{B} [b'_i]^{B'}=[b'_i]^B = b'_i. \end{equation*}
That is, \([\id_V]_{B'}^B\) is the matrix whose columns are \(b_1', \dots, b_n'\text{.}\)
