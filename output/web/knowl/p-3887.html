By Sylow's Theorem we know the following: - \(n_3|25\) and \(n_{3}\equiv1\mod{3}\text{,}\) so our options are \(1\) and \(25\text{.}\) - \(n_5|3\) and \(n_{3}\equiv1\mod{5}\text{,}\) so there can only be one. Let \(P\) denote the unique Sylow \(5\)-subgroup, which has order \(25\) and is normal in \(G\text{.}\) As \(P\) is a group of order \(p^2\) we know that it is abelian. Thus there are only two options for \(P\) up to isomorphism: \(\Z_5\times\Z_5\) and \(\Z_{25}\text{.}\)
