Let \(F\) be a field. Given a basis \(B\) of an \(F\)-vector space \(V\) and vector \(w \in V\text{,}\) let
\begin{equation*} w = a_1 v_1 + \cdots + a_n v_n, \end{equation*}
for \(n \geq 0\text{,}\) \(0 \ne a_i \in F\) and \(v_i \in B\) for all \(i\text{,}\) be the unique way of writing \(w\) as a linear combination of some of the members of \(B\) using non-zero coefficients. Then for any \(1 \leq i \leq n\text{,}\) we have that
\begin{equation*} B' := (B \setminus \{v_i\}) \cup \{w\} \end{equation*}
is also a basis of \(V\text{.}\)
