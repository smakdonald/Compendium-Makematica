Let \(P_n\) denote the the \(\R\)-vector space of polynomials of degree at most \(n\) (including the zero polynomial) and consider the linear transformation \(\phi :P_3\to P_2\) given by \(\phi(f)=f'\text{,}\) i.e.Â taking the derivative. Take \(B=\{1,x,x^2,x^3\}\) for an ordered basis of \(P_3\) and \(C = \{1,x,x^2\}\) for an ordered basis of \(P_2\text{.}\) Then for \(p(x) = 5 + 3x + 2x^2 - 7x^3\) we have
\begin{equation*} [p(x)]^B = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ -7\end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} [\phi]_B^C= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 &0 & 0 \\ 0 &0 & 2 & 0 \\ 0& 0& 0& 3 \\ \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
We have
\begin{equation*} [\phi]_B^C \cdot [p(x)]^B = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \\ -21 \end{bmatrix} = [p'(x)]^C = [\phi(p(x))]^C \end{equation*}
confirming in this example one of the assertions of the Lemma.
