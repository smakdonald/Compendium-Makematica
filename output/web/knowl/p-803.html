Suppose \(G\) is a group with normal subgroups \(H\mathrel{\unlhd}G\) and \(K\mathrel{\unlhd}G\) such that \(H\cap K=\{e\}\text{.}\) Then the following hold:
  1. \(HK\cong H\times K\) via the isomorphism of groups \(\theta: H \times K \to HK\) defined by \(\theta(h,k) = hk\text{.}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle H\cong \theta^{-1}(H)=\{(h,e)\mid h\in H\}\leq H\times K\)
  3. \(K\cong \theta^{-1}(K)=\{(e,k)\mid k\in K\}\leq H\times K\text{.}\)
