In case (2), \(\# \operatorname{Aut}(C_2 \times C_2) = \# \operatorname{GL}_2(\mathbb{F}_2) = 6\) and so there is a unique subgroup of order \(3\text{.}\) This leads to three possibities for \(\rho\text{,}\) including the trivial one. The two non-trivial maps have conjugate (in fact, equal) images. By the homework problem, we get just two possible groups: one is the abelian group \(C_2 \times C_2 \times C_3\) and the other is non-abelian.
