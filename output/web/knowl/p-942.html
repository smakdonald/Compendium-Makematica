Let \(G\) be a group of order 6. Cayley’s theorem gives that there exist elements \(x,y\in G\) with \(|x|=2\) and \(|y|=3\text{.}\) Let \(K=\langle x \rangle\) and \(H=\langle y\rangle\text{.}\) Since \([G:H]=2\text{,}\) \(H\) is a normal subgroup of \(G\) and since \(H\cap K\) is a common subgroup of \(H\) and \(K\) Lagrange’s theorem gives that \(|H\cap K|\mid \operatorname{gcd}(|H|,|K|)=1\text{.}\) Thus \(H\cap K=\{e\}\) and since \(|HK|=\frac{|H||K|}{|H\cap K|}=6=|G|\) we deduce that \(HK=G\text{.}\) Proposition [cross-reference to target(s) "prop:internaldirprod" missing or not unique] now gives that \(G\) is the internal semidirect product of \(H\) and \(K\text{.}\) More to the point, \(G\cong H\rtimes_\rho K\text{,}\) where \(\rho:K\to \operatorname{Aut}(H)\) gives the action of \(K\) on \(H\) by conjugation.
