Solution 10.4.1.

For example, in \(\R[x]\text{,}\) the set \(S\) of polynomials for which every term has even degree is a subring (it's closed under subtraction and multiplication), but it is not an ideal because it is not closed under multiplication by arbitrary polynomials. Indeed, \(p(x)=x^2\in S\text{,}\) but \(xp(x)=x^3\not\in S\text{.}\)
