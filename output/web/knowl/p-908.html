Suppose by way of contradiction that \(G\) is simple. Thus \(n_3\neq 1\text{,}\) so \(n_3=7\text{.}\) Let \(G\) act on the \(\Syl_3(G)\) by conjugation, yielding the homomorphism
\begin{equation*} \rho: G\to S_7 \end{equation*}
granted via the permutation representation. By (2) in Sylow's Theorem we see that \(\rho\) is not trivial. As \(|S_7|=7!\) we see that \(|G|\not\big||S_7|\text{,}\) meaning that the \(\ker(\rho)\) is non-trivial, yielding a non-trivial normal subgroup of \(G\text{,}\) a contradiction.
