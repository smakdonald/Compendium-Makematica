Consider the polynomial \(f(x)=x^{p}-\a^p\in F[x]\text{,}\) and notice that \(f'(x)=px^{p-1}=0\text{,}\) as we are in a field of characteristic \(p\text{.}\) However, this characteristic also yields \(x^p-\a^p=(x-\a)^p\text{.}\) As \(F\) is a field we have \(F[x]\) as a UFD, and thus as \(x-\a\) is irreducible in \(F[x]\) it is also prime. Therefore this is the unique factorization of \(f\) up to associates. If \(f\) was reducible \(F\) it would thus have to be divisible into power os \(x-\a\text{,}\) which will never be reducible as \(\a\not\in F\text{.}\) Thus \(f\) is irreducible in \(F[x]\text{,}\) making it the minimal polynomial of \(\a\text{.}\) However, if \(|\Aut(L/F )|= [L : F ]\) this would make \(L\) the splitting field of \(f\) over \(F\text{,}\) which it is not, given \(\a\not\in F\text{.}\) Thus \(|\Aut(L/F )| < [L : F ]\text{.}\) #### Problem 5 #unfinished Let F be a field, and let \(f (x) ∈ F [x]\text{.}\) Recall that \(f (x)\) is separable provided, for every extension field \(K/F , f (x)\) has no multiple roots in \(K\text{.}\) (A multiple root is an element \(α ∈ K\) such that \((x − α)^2 | f (x)\) in \(K[x].)\)
