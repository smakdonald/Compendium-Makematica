Notice that \(K\) is an extension of degree \(24\text{,}\) and thus by the FTGT we have a subgroup \(H\leq G\) such that \(H=\Gal(L/K)\text{.}\) As \([L:\Q]=[L:K][K:\Q]\) we have \(8=[L:K]\cdot 4\text{,}\) and thus \(|H|=2\text{.}\) This makes \(H\) the cyclic subgroup of \(D_8\) generated by a reflection, the only element of order \(2\) in \(D_8\text{,}\) making it unique. This is also a normal subgroup of \(D_8\text{,}\) making \(K\) Galois over \(\Q\text{.}\)
