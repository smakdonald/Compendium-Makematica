Let \(W\) be a subspace of a vector space \(V\text{.}\) Show that \(\dim(V) = \dim(W) + \dim(V/W)\text{.}\) #### Problem – Let \(F\) be a field, \(f\!: V \to W\) be an \(F\)-linear transformation, and \(\coker(f):=W/\im(f)\text{.}\) Prove that
\begin{equation*} \dim(\coker(f)) + \dim(V) = \dim(W) + \dim(\ker(f)). \end{equation*}
#### Problem – Let \(\phi: V \to V\) be an \(F\)-linear transformation. Prove that if \(\phi \circ \phi = 0\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \dim(\im(\phi))) \leq \frac{1}{2} \dim(V). \end{equation*}
#### Problem 4 #unfinished Let \(V\) be a subspace of a finite-dimensional vector space, \(W\text{.}\) Recall that a subspace \(U\) of \(W\) is called a complement of \(V\) if \(U\oplus V = W\text{.}\) Prove the following statements.
  1. Every complement of \(V\) has dimension \(\dim W-\dim V\) .
  2. If \(V\) is not \(0\) or \(W\text{,}\) then \(V\) has more than one complement.
  3. If \(T\) is a subspace of \(W\) with \(\dim T + \dim V > \dim W\) , then \(T\cap V\) is non-zero.
