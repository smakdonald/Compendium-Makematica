To see this, set \(G= \Gal(L/F)\text{.}\) Then \(|G| = 21 = 3 \cdot 7\) and by the Sylow theorems, there is a unique Sylow \(7\)-subgroup, call it \(H\text{,}\) and hence \(H\) is normal in \(G\text{.}\) It follows from the Fundamental Theorem that \(E := L^H\) is an intermediate field that
  1. is Galois over \(F\) and
  2. satisfies \([E: F] = [G :H] = 3\text{.}\) Moreover, it is unique since \(G\) has just one subgroup of index \(3\text{.}\)
