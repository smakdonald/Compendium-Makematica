Section I: Group Theory

Problem F.28. Problem 1.

Let \(p\) be a prime and \(H\) a finite \(p\)-group.
  1. (a) Suppose \(H\) acts on the finite set \(S\text{,}\) and let \(S_0\) be the fixed points of the action, i.e.,
    \begin{equation*} S_0 = \{x \in S | hx = x \text{ for all }h \in H\}. \end{equation*}
    Prove that \(|S| \equiv |S_0|\mod{p}\text{.}\)
  2. (b) Suppose \(G\) is a finite group and \(H \leq G\) (where \(H\) is still a finite \(p\)-group). Prove that \([N_G(H) : H]\equiv[G : H]\mod{p}).\)
Solution.

Problem F.29. Problem 2 (*).

A normal subgroup \(H\) of \(G\) is called a direct factor of \(G\) if there is some normal subgroup \(K\) of \(G\) such that \(G = H \times K\) (internal direct product).
  1. If \(H\) is a direct factor of \(G\text{,}\) \(L\) is any group, and \(\phi : H \to L\) is any group homomorphism, prove that \(\phi\) extends to a homomorphism \(\widetilde{\phi} : G \to L\) such that \(\widetilde\phi(h) = \phi(h)\) for all \(h \in H\text{.}\)
  2. Provide a counter-example (with justification) to the previous part if \(H\) is assumed to be a normal subgroup of \(G\text{,}\) but not a direct factor. Hint: One such example involves \(G = \Z\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.33. Problem 6.

Let \(L\) be a finite Galois field extension of \(\Q\text{.}\) Let \(E\) and \(F\) be subfields of \(L\) such that \(EF = L, E/\Q\) is normal, and \(E\cap F = \Q\text{.}\) Prove that \([L : Q] = [E : Q][F : Q].\)
Solution.

Problem F.36. Problem 9.

Let \(I\) be an ideal in a commutative ring \(R\text{,}\) let \(M\) and \(N\) be \(R\)-modules and let \(f : M \to N\) be an \(R\)-module homomorphism.
  1. Prove there is a unique \(R\)-module homomorphism \(\ov{f} : M/IM \to N/IN\) such that \(\ov{f} \circ p = q \circ f\text{,}\) where \(p : M\to M/IM\) and \(q : N\to N/IN\) are the canonical quotient maps
  2. Prove that if \(I^2 = 0\) and \(\ov{f}\) is surjective, then so is \(f\) . (Recall that \(I^2\) is the ideal generated by all elements of the form \(ab\text{,}\) where \(a, b \in I\text{.}\))
Solution.
