Let \(R\) be a ring and let \(M\) and \(N\) be \(R\)-modules. Then \(\Hom_R(M, N )\) denotes the set of all \(R\)-module homomorphisms from \(M\) to \(N\text{,}\) and \(\End_R(M)\) denotes the set \(\Hom_R(M, M)\text{.}\) We call \(\End(M)\) the endomorphism ring of \(M\) , and elements of \(\End(M)\) are called endomorphisms of \(M\text{.}\)
