Note that
\begin{equation*} C(x)=[0], C(x^2)= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0\\ 1 & 0\\ \end{bmatrix}, C(x)\oplus C(x^2)\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 1 & 0\\ \end{bmatrix}, \textrm{ and } C(x^3)= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0\\ 1 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 1 & 0\\ \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
As \(C(x)\oplus C(x^2)\) has rank 1 and \(C(x^3)\) has rank 2, since \(\rank(A)=\rank(B)\) we see that they must have the same invariant factors, making them similar.
