First, suppose that \(L=F(\a)\text{.}\) As \(L\) is a Galois extension the minimum polynomial of \(\a\) in \(F\) splits completely into linear factors. Thus \(G\) acts faithfully on the roots of \(\mp_\a(x)\text{,}\) which includes \(\a\text{.}\) Thus the images of \(\a\) under elements of \(G\) are distinct.
