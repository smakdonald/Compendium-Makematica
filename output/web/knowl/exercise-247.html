Exploration 6.35.

Similarity of two-by-two matrices:
  1. Let \(F\) be any field and \(A, B \in \Mat_{2 \times 2}(F)\text{,}\) and assume that neither \(A\) nor \(B\) is a scalar matrix. (Recall that a scalar matrix is one of the form \(c I_n\) for some \(c \in F\text{.}\)) Prove \(A\) and \(B\) are similar if and only if they have the same determinant and the same trace.
  2. Let \(F\) be a finite field with \(q\) elements. Find, with justification, the number of similarity classes of \(2 \times 2\) matrices with entries in \(F\text{.}\)
  3. Let \(F\) be a finite field with \(q\) elements. Find, with justification, the number of conjugacy classes of the group \(GL_2(F)\text{.}\)
