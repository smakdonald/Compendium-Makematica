Then \(z \in L\) is a root of the polynomial \(x^p - y \in F[x]\text{.}\) Moreover since \(F\) is the field of fractions of the PID \(R = (\Z/p)[y]\) and \(y\) is a prime element of \(R\text{,}\) we may apply Eisenstein (and Gauss) to conclude that \(x^p - y\) is irreducible in \(F[x]\text{.}\) This proves that \(m_{z, F}(x) = x^p - y\text{.}\) This polynomial is not separable since in \(L[x]\) it is equal to \((x-z)^p\) and hence has a repeated root. (Or, you may use that its derivative is \(0\text{.}\))
