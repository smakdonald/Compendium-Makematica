Let \(G\) be a finite group of order \(p^2q\) with \(p < q\) prime numbers, and suppose by way of contradiction that \(G\) is simple. By Sylow's Theorem we have the following: - \(n_p|q\) and \(n_p\cong 1\mod{p}\text{,}\) and thus \(n_p=q\text{.}\) - \(n_q|p^2\) and \(n_q\cong 1\mod{q}\text{,}\) and thus \(n_q=p^2\text{.}\) From this information we see that there are \(p^2(q-1)\) elements of order \(q\) and \((p^2-1)q\) elements of order \(p\text{,}\) for a lovely total of \(p^2q-p^2+p^2q-q\) elements, which is too many.
