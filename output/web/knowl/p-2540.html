Suppose \(A\) and \(B\) are subfields of a field extension \(K/F\) with \([A : F ]\) and \([B : F ]\) both finite. Let \(E\) be the subfield of K generated by \(A\) and \(B\text{.}\)
  1. Show that \([E : F ] \leq [A : F ][B : F ]\)
  2. Prove that equality holds when \([A : F ]\) and \([B : F ]\) are relatively prime.
  3. Prove there are two subfields of \(\R/\Q\text{,}\) \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) neither contained in the other, so that the inequality in part (1) is strict.
