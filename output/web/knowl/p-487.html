By Sylow's Theorem we know the number of Sylow \(23\)-subgroups of \(G\) must divide \(35\) and be congruent to \(1\mod{23}\text{,}\) the only option of which is \(1\text{.}\) Let \(H\) denote the unique Sylow \(23\)-subgroup and let \(K\) be the cyclic subgroup generated by the element of order \(35\text{.}\)
