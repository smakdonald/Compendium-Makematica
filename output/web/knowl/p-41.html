  1. Trivial Subgroup.
    The trivial subgroup is the group with a single element \(\{e\}\text{.}\)
  2. \((\Z,+), (\Q,+), (\R,+),\) and \((\C,+)\) each form an abelian group, where \(+\) denotes traditional addition.
  3. \(\Q^+\) and \(\R^+\) form groups under traditional multiplication.
  4. General Linear Group.
    For any positive integer \(n\text{,}\) let
    \begin{equation*} \operatorname{GL}_n(\C)=\{ \text{invertible } n \times n \;\text{matrices with entries in}\; \C\}. \end{equation*}
    By invertible I mean those matrices that have two-sided inverses, but it turns out that if an \(n \times n\) matrix has a left inverse then it it is automatically a right inverse too, and vice versa. Then \(\operatorname{GL}_n(\C)\) is a non-abelian group under matrix multiplication, known as the general linear group. 2 
  5. For any \(n\in\N\text{,}\) let \(\Z/n\) denote the The Integers Modulo \(n\) (*). Then \((\Z/n,+)\) forms an abelian group where \(+\) denotes addition modulo \(n\text{.}\)
  6. The subset \(L=\{1,-1,i,-i\}\) of the complex numbers forms an abelian group under multiplication.
