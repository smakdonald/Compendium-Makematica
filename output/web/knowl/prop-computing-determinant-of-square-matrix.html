Proposition 4.54.

Let \(R\) be any non-zero commutative ring. Let \(A\) be a square matrix and let \(B\) be a matrix obtained form \(A\) by a single elementary column operation: 1. If the operation is of type I, \(\det(B) = \det(A)\text{.}\) 2. If the operation is of type II, given by multiplying a column of \(A\) by a unit \(u\text{,}\) then \(\det(B)=u\det(A)\text{.}\) 3. If the operation is of type III, \(\det(B)=-\det(A)\text{.}\)
