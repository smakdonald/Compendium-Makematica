If two elements of \(S_n\) are conjugate, say \(\beta = \sigma\alpha \sigma^{-1}\text{,}\) then they have the same cycle type, since we may write \(\alpha\) as a product of disjoint cycles \(\alpha = \alpha_1 \cdots \alpha_m\) and then apply Lemma 2.27. Indeed, \(\sigma\alpha \sigma^{-1} = (\sigma\alpha_1 \sigma^{-1}) \cdots (\sigma\alpha_m \sigma^{-1})\) and the Lemma 2.27 shows that the right-side is a product of disjoint cycles.
