By solving for \(v_1\) (as we may since \(a_1 \ne 0\)) we obtain
\begin{equation*} v_1 = b_1 w + \sum_{j \geq 2} b_j v_j \end{equation*}
for some \(b_j \in F\) and hence \(v_1 \in \Span(B')\text{.}\) Given any \(u \in V\text{,}\) since \(B\) spans, we have \(u = \sum_i c_i u_i\) for some \(c_i \in F, u_i \in B\text{.}\) If one of \(u_i\)'s is equal to \(v_1\text{,}\) then we may use the previous equation to replace the term \(c_i u_i\) with a linear combination of members of \(B'\text{.}\) This proves that \(u \in \Span(B')\text{.}\) So \(B'\) spans \(V\text{.}\)
