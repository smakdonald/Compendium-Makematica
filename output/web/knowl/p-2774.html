Let \(E\) and \(K\) be finite splitting fields over \(\Q\text{.}\) So there exist polynomials \(f,g\in F[x]\) such that \(f\) splits in \(F\) and \(g\) splits in \(E\text{.}\) Note that both \(E\) and \(F\) are finite extensions, as each can be reached by adjoining each of the roots of \(g\) and \(f\text{,}\) respectfully.
