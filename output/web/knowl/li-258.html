Item 4.a.
Let \(G\) act on \(H\) by Conjugation. Thus the associated permutation homomorphism \(\rho\) is defined such that \(\rho(g)=\phi_g\text{,}\) where \(\phi_g(h)=ghg\inv\text{.}\) Notice that when \(x\in C_G(H)\) we have \(\phi_x(h)=xhx\inv=h\) for all \(h\in H\text{,}\) and thus \(x\in\ker(\rho)\text{.}\) If \(x\in\ker(\rho)\) then \(xhx\inv=h\) for all \(h\in H\text{,}\) and thus \(x\in C_G(H)\text{.}\) Thus \(C_G(H)=\ker(\rho)\) and \(C_G(H)\nsg N_G(H)\text{.}\)
