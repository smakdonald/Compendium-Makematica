Since \(f\) is a ring homomorphism, it is in particular a group homomorphism \((R,+)\to (S,+)\text{.}\) We know the kernel of a group homomorphism is a subgroup, so \(\ker(f)\leq (S,+)\text{.}\) All that remains to be shown is that for any \(r\in R\) \(r\ker(f)\subseteq \ker(f)\) and \(\ker(f)r\subseteq \ker(f)\text{.}\) Let \(x\in \ker(f)\text{;}\) then \(f(x)=0\) and \(f(rx)=f(r)f(x)=0\text{,}\) \(f(xr)=f(x)f(r)=0\) show \(rx,xr\in \ker(f)\text{.}\)
