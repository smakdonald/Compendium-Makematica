Solution 4.3.5.1.
Let \(u \in X\text{.}\) As the action is transitive, by the exercise above we have that the kernel of the action is the intersection of \(gG_ug\inv\) over all \(g \in G\text{.}\) However, as \(G\) is abelian, \(gG_ug\inv = G_u\) for all \(g \in G\text{.}\) Hence, the kernel of the action is \(G_u\) for any \(u \in X\text{.}\) On the other hand, the action is faithful, which means the kernel of the action is the identity. It follows that \(G_u = \{e\}\) for every \(u \in X\text{.}\) Finally, as the action is transitive, \(X = \Orb(u)\) for some (any) \(u \in X\text{.}\) By the Orbit-Stabilizer theorem, \(|X|= |\Orb(u)|= [G : G_u] = |G|\text{.}\)
