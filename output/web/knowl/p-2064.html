Some books write \(\operatorname{Gal}(L / F)\) for \(\operatorname{Aut}(L / F)\text{,}\) and call it the Galois group of \(L\) over \(F\text{.}\) We will reserve that notation for a special type of finite extensions - those that are Galois - and use only \(\operatorname{Aut}(L / F)\) for the general case.
