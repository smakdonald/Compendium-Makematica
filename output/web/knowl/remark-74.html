Remark 15.9.

For non-commutative rings, there is a version of the previous remark that remains true: Replace the vertical map on the left in diagram with the map given by \(v \mapsto (v^\tau A^\tau)^\tau\) where the exponent \(\tau\) denotes taking the transpose of a matrix. (If \(R\) is commutative, we have \((AB)^\tau = (BA)^\tau\) for all matrices \(A\) and \(B\) of an appropriate size, and thus \((v^\tau A^\tau)^\tau = (A^\tau)^\tau (v^\tau)^\tau = Av\text{.}\) When \(R\) is not commutative, it is {} true in general that \((AB)^\tau = (BA)^\tau\text{.}\))
