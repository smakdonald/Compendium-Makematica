Theorem 1.12. Properties of (Semi)Groups.

  1. Unique Identity.
    In a monoid \(G\text{,}\) the element \(e\) satisfying the Idenity axiom of Definition 1.2 is unique, and we thus refer to it as the identity element of \(G\text{.}\)
  2. Unique Inverses.
    In a group \(G\text{,}\) for each \(x\text{,}\) the element \(y\) satisfying the Inverses axiom of Definition 1.7 is unique, and we thus refer to it as the the inverse of \(x\) and we will henceforth denote it by \(x^{-1}\text{.}\)
  3. Cancellation.
    In a group \(G\text{,}\) if \(xy = xz\) then \(y=z\text{.}\) Similarly, if \(yx = zx\) we have \(y = z\text{.}\)
