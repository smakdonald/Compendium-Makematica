Item 2.
Let \(I\) denote any indexing set and for each \(i \in I\text{,}\) let \(H_i\) be a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) We need to prove
\begin{equation*} H := \bigcap_{i \in I} H_i \end{equation*}
is a subgroup. It's not empty since \(e_G \in H_i\) for all \(i\text{.}\) If \(x,y \in G\text{,}\) then for each \(i\text{,}\) \(x,y \in H_i\) and hence \(xy^{-1} \in H_i\text{.}\) It follows that \(xy^{-1} \in H\text{.}\)
