Proposition 4.21. Finding Unique Equivalent Matrix.

Let \(R\) be a non-zero commutative ring, let \(V\) be a free \(R\)-module of dimension \(n\text{,}\) and let \(B = \{b_1, \dots, b_n\}\) be any ordered basis of \(V\text{.}\) If \(P\) is any \(n \times n\) matrix with entries in \(R\) that is invertible (i.e., there is another \(n \times n\) matrix \(P^{-1}\) such that \(PP^{-1} = I_n = P^{-1}P\)), then \(P = [\id_V]_B^C\) for a unique basis \(C\) of \(V\text{.}\)
