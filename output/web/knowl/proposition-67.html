Proposition 2.21.

Let \(R\) be a non-zero commutative ring and suppose \(p: R^m \to R^n\) is a surjective homomorphism of \(R\)-modules for some non-negative integers \(m\) and \(n\text{.}\)
  1. Prove that if \(R\) is a field, then \(m \geq n\text{.}\)
  2. Prove that if \(R\) is any non-zero commutative ring, then \(m \geq n\text{.}\)
