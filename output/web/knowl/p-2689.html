However, as \(g^k=1\) for all \(g\in G\) we know that \(g\) is the root of the polynomial \(f(x)=x^k-1\) in \(F[x]\text{.}\) By the [[Mathematics/Number Theory/Results/Lemma - Factor Theorem|Factor Theorem]] there are thus at most \(k\) roots of \(f\text{,}\) but there are \(n\) distinct roots. Thus \(k=n\text{.}\)
