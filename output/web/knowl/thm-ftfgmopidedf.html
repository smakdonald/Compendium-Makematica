Theorem 16.32. FTFGMOPIDEDF.

Let \(R\) be a PID and let \(M\) be a finitely generated \(R\)-module. Then there exist integers \(r \geq 0, k \geq 0\text{,}\) prime elements \(p_1,\ldots,p_s\) of \(R\) (not necessarily distinct), and integers \(e_1,\ldots ,e_s \geq 1\text{,}\) such that
\begin{equation*} M \cong R^r \oplus R/(p_1^{e_1}) \oplus \cdots \oplus R/(p_s^{e_s}). \end{equation*}
Moreover \(r\) and \(k\) are uniquely determined by \(M\text{,}\) and the list \(p_1^{e_1},\ldots, p_s^{e_s}\) is unique up to associates and reordering.

Proof.
