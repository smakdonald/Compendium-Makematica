Recall that submodules of \(R\) are left ideals and the simple ones are the minimal (nonzero) left ideals. So, \(R\) is left semisimple if and only if \(R\) is the internal direct sum of some collection of minimal left ideals \(I_j\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} R = \bigoplus_{j \in J} I_j. \end{equation*}
Moreover, \(R\) is f.g. as a module over itself, and so this must be a finite direct sum. So, \(R\) is left semisimple if and only if \(R\) decomposes as an internal direct sum of the form \(R = I_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus I_m\) for some finite collection \(I_1, \dots, I_m\) of minimal left ideals.
