Let \(G\) be a finite group and \(p\) a prime dividing the order of \(G\text{.}\) Let P be a Sylow \(p\)-subgroup. (a) Suppose \(Q\) is a Sylow \(p\)-subgroup distinct from \(P\text{.}\) Prove that \(PQ\)[^1] is not a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) (Recall that \(PQ = {pq | p ∈ P, q ∈ Q}\text{.}\)) (b) Prove that \(N_G(P ) = N_G(N_G(P ))\) where \(N_G(H)\) denotes the normalizer in \(G\) of a subgroup \(H\text{.}\)
