Let \(R\) be a ring and let \(M\) and \(N\) be \(R\)-modules. An \(R\)-module homomorphism from \(M\) to \(N,\) sometimes called an \(R\)-map, is a function \(h: M \to N\) such that for all \(r \in R\) and \(m,n \in M\) we have
  1. \(h(m+n)=h(m)+h(n)\text{,}\) i.e. \(h\) is an additive group homomorphism, and
  2. \(h(rm) = rh(m)\text{.}\)
