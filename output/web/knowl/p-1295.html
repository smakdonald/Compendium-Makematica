Let \(R\) be a commutative integral domain and \(K\) its field of fractions. Let \(a\) and \(b\) be nonzero elements of \(R\text{,}\) such that \((a) \cap(b) = (ab)\text{.}\) Let \(f : R[x] \to K\) be the unique ring homomorphism with \(f (r) = \frac{r}{1}\) for \(r \in R\) and \(f (x) = \frac{b}{a}\text{.}\) Prove that a polynomial \(p(x) \in R[x]\) satisfies \(f(p(x))=0\) if and only if \(p(x) = (ax - b)q(x)\) for some polynomial \(q(x) \in R[x]\text{.}\) (Hint: one way is to use induction on deg(p(x)).)
