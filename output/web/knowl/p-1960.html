Prove that if \(R\) is a field, \(V\) is a subspace (i.e., submodule) of \(W\text{,}\) and \(i\) is the inclusion map, then \(i\) is a split injection. Note that since \(i\) is the inclusion map, what you need to prove is that there exists an \(R\)-module homomorphism \(q: W \to V\) such that \(q(v) = v\text{.}\) {}: Start by picking a basis \(B\) of \(V\) and use a theorem proven in class to show that \(B\) can be extended to a basis \(C\) of \(W\text{.}\) Use \(C\) to construct \(q\text{.}\) (I am assuming that \(V\) is a subspace of \(W\) and that \(i\) is the inclusion map just for simplicity â€” more generally, it is true that every injective \(R\)-module homomorphism is a split injection whenever \(R\) is a field.)
