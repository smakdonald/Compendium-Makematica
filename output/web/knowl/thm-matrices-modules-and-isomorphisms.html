Theorem 15.4. Matrices, Modules, and Isomorphisms.

Let \(R\) be a non-zero commutative ring and let \(A \in \Mat_{m \times n}(R)\) and \(B \in Mat_{m' \times n'}(R)\) for some \(m,n,m',n' \geq 1\text{.}\) Then \(A\) and \(B\) present isomorphic \(R\)-modules if \(B\) can be obtained from \(A\) by any finite sequence of operations of the following form:
  1. an elementary row operation,
  2. an elementary column operation,
  3. deletion of the \(j\)-th column and \(i\)-th row of a matrix whose \(j\)-th column is the vector \(e_i\text{,}\)
  4. the reverse of (3),
  5. deletion of a column of all \(0\)'s,
  6. the reverse of (5).
