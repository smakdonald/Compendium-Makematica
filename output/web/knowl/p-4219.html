Part (a) Suppose \(G\) has an element of order \(25\text{.}\) Let \(H\) be the cyclic subgroup generated by this element, and notice that \([G:H]=3\text{,}\) the smallest prime dividing the order of \(G\text{.}\) Thus \(H\nsg G\text{.}\) Let \(K\) be a Sylow \(3\)-subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) Notice that this group has order \(3\) and is thus cyclic as well. Thus we have \(G\cong H\sdp_{\rho}K\text{,}\) where \(\rho:K\to\Aut(H)\) is a homomorphism. Note that \(\Aut(H)\cong\Z^\times_{25}\text{,}\) which is cyclic of order \(20\text{.}\) Since \(|K| = 3\) and \(\gcd(3,20) = 1\text{,}\) we conclude that \(\rho\) must be the trivial map. Hence, \(G = H\times K\text{,}\) which is a direct product of cyclic groups of relatively prime order. Hence, \(G\) is cyclic.
