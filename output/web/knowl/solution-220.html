Solution H.78.1.

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring (with \(1\)) and let \(M\) be an \(R\)-module such that \(M\neq 0\) and the only submodules of \(M\) are \(0\) and \(M\text{.}\)
As \(M\neq 0\text{,}\) there exists a non-zero element \(m\in M\text{.}\) Let \(f:R\to M\) be defined by \(f(r)=rm\text{.}\) Since \(1\in R\) and \(1m=m\in Rm\text{,}\) we have \(Rm=M\text{.}\) By the [[Theorem â€“ Module Isomorphism Theorems|First Isomorphism Theorem]] we see that \(R/\ker(f)\cong M\text{,}\) making \(\ker(f)\) an ideal in \(R\text{,}\) which we shall conspicuously denote as \(I\) henceforth. By the Lattice Isomorphism Theorem the only two ideals of \(R/I\) are \(0\) and \(R/I\text{,}\) making \(R/I\) a field, and \(I\) a maximal ideal in \(R\text{.}\)
