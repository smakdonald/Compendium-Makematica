Theorem 1.59.

For any field \(F\text{,}\) there exists an [[Mathematics/Definitions/Algebraic Closure|algebraic closure]] of \(F\text{.}\) If \(L\) and \(L'\) are two algebraic closures of the same field \(F\text{,}\) then there exists a field isomorphism \(\phi: L \xra{\cong} L'\) such that \(\phi|_F = \id_F\) (i.e., \(\phi(a) = a\) for all \(a \in F\)).

Proof.
