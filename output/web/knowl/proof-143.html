Proof

Let \(K/F\) be a finite extension of fields such that the degree \([K : F ]\) is odd.
Notice that \(F(b^2)\subseteq F(b)\) as everything in \(b^2\) can be written in terms of \(b\text{.}\) Suppose by way of contradiction that there exists some \(\a\in F(b)\) such that \(\a\not\in F(b^2)\text{.}\) Then \(b\not\in F(b^2)\text{.}\) Notice that \(b\) is a root of the polynomial \(f(x)=x^2-b^2\in F(b^2)\text{,}\) which is irreducible in \(F(b^2)\) as it is a degree \(2\) polynomial that has no roots in \(F(b^2)\text{.}\) [provisional cross-reference: empty] Thus \(f\) is the minimum polynomial of \(b\) and \([F(b):F(b^2)]=2.\) [provisional cross-reference: empty]
However, by the Degree Formula [provisional cross-reference: empty] \([K:F]=[K:F(b)][F(b):F(b^2)][F(b^2):F]\text{,}\) which is a problem, given \(2\) now divides \([K:F]\text{,}\) an odd number. Thus \(F (b) = F (b^2)\text{.}\)
