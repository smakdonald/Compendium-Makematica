We show that both \(x\) and \((x^{-1})^{-1}\) are inverses of the element \(x\inv\text{.}\) First, recall from Definition 1.6 that \(x\cdot x\inv=e\text{.}\) However, it is also the case that \((x\inv)\inv\cdot x\inv=e\text{.}\) By part (2) of Theorem 1.7 we know that inverses are unique, and thus it must be the case that \((x^{-1})^{-1} = x\text{.}\)
