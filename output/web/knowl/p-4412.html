Let \(k\) be the LCM of all orders of elements in \(G\text{.}\) Then \(g^k=1\) and thus \(g\) is a root of the polynomial \(x^k-1\) for all \(g\in G\text{.}\) By Lagrange's Theorem every element divides \(n\text{,}\) and so we have \(k\leq n\text{.}\) However, by the Factor Theorem the polynomial \(x^k-1\) can have at most \(k\) roots, and we have \(n\) distinct elements, and thus we have \(k=n\text{.}\) Thus there must exist an element of order \(n\) in \(G\text{,}\) making \(G\) cyclic, as desired.
