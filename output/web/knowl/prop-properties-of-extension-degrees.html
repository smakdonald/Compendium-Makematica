Proposition 1.14.

Assume \(F\) is a field and \(p(x) = \sum_i a_i x^i \in F[x]\) is an irreducible polynomial. Set \(L = F[x]/(p(x))\text{,}\) and for \(f \in F[x]\text{,}\) let \(\ov{f}\) denote the coset \(f(x) + (p(x)) \in L\text{.}\) The following hold:
  1. \(F \into L\) is a field extension via the map given by \(a \mapsto \ov{a}\) for \(a \in F\text{.}\) (This is technically an injective homomorphisms of fields.)
  2. \([L:F]=\deg(p)\text{.}\)
  3. \(p(x)\) has a root in \(L\text{;}\) in fact, the element \(\ov{x} \in L\) is a root of this polynomial: \(\sum_i \ov{a_i} \cdot \ov{x}^i = 0 \in L\text{.}\)

Proof.
