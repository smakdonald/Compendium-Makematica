Proof

We prove the contra-positive. Suppose \(f\) is reducible in \(R[x]\text{.}\) Then we have \(f(x) = g(x) h(x)\) for some monic, non-constant polynomials \(g\) and \(h\) in \(R[x]\text{.}\) It follows that \(\ov{f} = \ov{gh} = \ov{g} \ov{h}\) holds in \((R/p)[x]\text{.}\) But since \(g\) and \(h\) are monic, non-constant polynomials, \(\ov{g}\) and \(\ov{h}\) are both non-constant polynomials in \((R/p)[x]\) and hence \(\ov{f}\) is reducible in \((R/p)[x]\text{.}\)
