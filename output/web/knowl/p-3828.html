Proof. \((\Rightarrow)\) Suppose that \(g\) is diagonalizable. Thus there exists a change of basis matrix \(A\) such that \(A\) is diagonal. As it is diagonal, its diagonal entries are the eigenvalues of \(g\text{,}\) and thus the roots of the minimal polynomial of \(g\text{.}\) Using row and column operations we can rearrange \(A\) so that all repeated linear factors are next to each other in the diagonal, for convenience.
