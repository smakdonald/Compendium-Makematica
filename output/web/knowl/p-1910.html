If \(a,b \in J\text{,}\) then \(a \in I_n\) and \(b \in I_m\) for some \(n,m\) and hence there is a \(N\) such that \(a,b \in I_N\text{.}\) It follows that \(a \pm b \in I_N \subseteq J\text{.}\) Finally \(0 \in J\text{.}\)) Thus by assumption \(J\) is finitely generated, say \(J = Rx_1 + \cdots + Rx_m\) for some \(x_1, \dots, x_m \in J\text{.}\)
