Proposition 10.12. Homomorphisms and Ideals.

If \(f: R \to S\) is a ring homomorphism, then
  1. the image of \(f\) is a subring of \(S\) and
  2. the kernel of \(f\) is an ideal of \(R\text{.}\)
  3. \(f\) is injective if and only if \(\ker(f)=\{0\}\text{.}\)
  4. if \(I\) is an ideal of \(R\) then \(f(I)\) is an ideal of \(f(R)\text{.}\)
  5. if \(J\) is an ideal of \(S\) then \(f^{-1}(J)\) is an ideal of \(R\text{.}\)
