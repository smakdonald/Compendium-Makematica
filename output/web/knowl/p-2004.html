Let \(R\) be a (not-necessarily commutative) ring let \(M\) be a left \(R\)-module. The annihilator of \(M\) in \(R\) is defined to be
\begin{equation*} \ann_R(M) = \{ r \in R \mid rm = 0 \text{ for all $m \in M$}\}. \end{equation*}
  1. Prove that \(\ann_R(M)\) is a \(2\)-sided ideal of \(R\text{.}\)
  2. Suppose \(M\) is an abelian group (i.e., a \(\Z\)-module) such that \(|M| = 400\) and \(\ann_\Z(M)\) is the ideal generated by \(20\text{.}\) How many possibilities, up to isomorphism, are there for \(M\text{?}\)
