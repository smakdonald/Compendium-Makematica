Remark 27.6.

As a consequence of the above exercise, a module is a finitely generated semisimple module if and only if it is a finite direct sum of simple modules. In this case if we write \(M=M_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus M_n\) as a sum of simple modules, there is a strict composition series
\begin{equation*} 0\subset M_1 \subset M_1 \oplus M_2 \subset \cdots \subset M_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus M_{n-1} \subset M \end{equation*}
so \(M\) has finite length, namely length \(n\text{,}\) and the composition factors are the modules \(M_i\text{.}\)
