  1. If \(H\) is a subgroup of \(G\text{,}\) then the left cosets of \(H\) in \(G\) correspond precisely to the orbits of \(H\) under the left regular action of \(G\) on itself.
  2. Two elements \(g,g' \in G\) are conjugate if and only if \(g\) and \(g'\) are in the same orbit of the conjugation action.
  3. \(C_G(g)\) is exactly the orbit of \(g\) under conjugation.
