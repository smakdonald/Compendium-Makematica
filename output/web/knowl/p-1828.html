Note that, in the context of this definition, we have \(F \subseteq F[\a] \subseteq F(\a) \subseteq L\text{,}\) with each inclusion being a subring inclusion. \(F, L\) and \(F(\a)\) are all fields and in general \(F[\a]\) is only a ring. But, in some cases we have \(F[\a] = F(\a)\) (and hence it is a field). Indeed, this occurs whenever \(\a\) is the root of some polynomial with coefficients in \(F\text{.}\)
