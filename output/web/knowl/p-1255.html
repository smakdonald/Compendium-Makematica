For any ring \(R\) and group \(G\text{,}\) we define the group ring \(R[G]\) as follows: As a set, \(R[G]\) is the free left \(R\)-module with basis \(G\text{;}\) that is,
\begin{equation*} R[G]=\left\{ \sum_g r_g g \mid r_g = 0_R \text{ for all by a finite number of }g's \right\}. \end{equation*}
We define addition as module addition; that is,
\begin{equation*} \left(\sum_g r_g g \right) + \left(\sum_h s_h h \right) = \sum_{f \in G} \left(r_f + s_f \right) f. \end{equation*}
Multiplication is the unique pairing that obeys the distributive laws and is such that \(R\) is a subring, \(1_RG\) is a subgroup of \((R[G]^\times, \cdot)\text{,}\) and every element of \(R\) commutes with every element of \(G\text{.}\) In general, we have
\begin{equation*} \left(\sum_g r_g g\right) \cdot \left(\sum_h s_h h \right) = \sum_{f \in G} \left(\sum_{\substack{(g,h) \in G \times G\\ gh= f}} r_gs_h\right) f. \end{equation*}
where the inner sum is over pairs of group elements whose product is \(f\text{.}\)
