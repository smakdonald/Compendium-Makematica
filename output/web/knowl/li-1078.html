Item 2.

Let \(p(x)\) be a non-constant irreducible polynomial of degree \(d\) in \(F[x]\text{.}\) Let \(K=F[x]/p(x)\text{.}\) Because \(p(x)\) is irreducible and \(F[x]\) is a PID, \((p(x))\) is a maximal ideal. Thus \(K = F[x]/(p(x))\) is a field (See: [[Theorem – Prime, Maximal, Domain, Field|Theorem]]), \([K:F]=d\text{,}\) and \(x+p(x)\) is a root of \(p(x)\) in \(K\) (See: [[Proposition – Properties of Extension Degrees|Proposition]]). Hence \(p(x)\) is no longer irreducible by Theorem 2.2.
