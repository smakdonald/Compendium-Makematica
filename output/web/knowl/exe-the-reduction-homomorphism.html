Exploration 9.62. Reduction Homomorphism.

Continuing with Example 9.61, we could have \(S = R/I\) for an ideal \(I\) of \(R\) and \(\phi\) could be the quotient map. Then
\begin{equation*} \tilde{\phi}: R[x_1, \dots, x_n] \to (R/I)[x_1, \dots, x_n] \end{equation*}
takes a polynomial and “reduces” its coefficients modulo \(I\text{.}\) We will usually denote the image of \(f(x)\) through the reduction homomorphism by \(\ov{f}(x)\text{.}\)
