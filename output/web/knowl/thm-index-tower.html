Theorem 3.9. The Index Tower.

Let \(G\) be a group (possibly infinite) and \(H \leq K\) subgroups of \(G\text{.}\) Suppose \([G : H]\) is finite. Prove that \([G : H] = [G : K][K : H].\) (Hint: First prove this in the case \(G\) is a finite group. Then prove you can reduce to the finite case.)
