Exercise 5. January 2012 (3).
Let \(G\) be a group of order \(90 = 2 · 3^2 · 5\) and let \(\Syl_3(G)\) denote the set of Sylow \(3\)-subgroups of \(G\text{.}\)
  1. Suppose for any \(Q, Q' \in \Syl_3(G)\) either \(Q = Q'\) or \(Q \cap Q' = {1}.\) Prove that \(G\) is not simple.
  2. Suppose there exists \(Q, Q' \in \Syl_3(G)\) such that \(|Q \cap Q'| = 3\text{.}\) Prove that \(G\) is not simple. (Hint: Consider the normalizer of \(Q \cap Q'\text{.}\))
