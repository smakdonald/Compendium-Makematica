Let \(R\) be a ring and let \(M\) be a left \(R\)-module. An \(R\)-submodule of \(M\) is a subset \(N \subseteq M\) such that
  1. \(N\) is a subgroup of \(M\) under \(+\) (so, we have \(0 \in N\text{,}\) if \(n \in N\) then \(-n \in N\text{,}\) and if \(n_1, n_2 \in N\) then \(n_1 + n_2 \in N\)), and
  2. \(rn \in N\) for all \(r \in R\) and \(n \in N\text{.}\)
