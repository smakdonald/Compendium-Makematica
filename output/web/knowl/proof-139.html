Proof

Given an \(R[G]\)-module structure on \(V\text{,}\) for every \(g\in G\text{,}\) there is a map \(m_g: V\to V\) given by \(v\mapsto g\cdot v\text{.}\) We have \(m_g (rv) = g (rv) = rg (v) = r m_g(v)\text{,}\) so \(m_g\) is \(R\)-linear. Moreover, the map \(\rho:G\to \operatorname{End}_R(V)\) that sends \(g\mapsto m_g\) preserves multiplication and identity: \(\rho(gh)(v) = gh v = g(hv) = \rho(g) \rho(h) (v)\) and \(\rho(e)(v) = v\text{.}\) Thus, we obtain an \(R\)-linear representation \(\rho:G\to \operatorname{Aut}_R(V)\text{.}\)
Conversely, recall that a module structure on an abelian group is equivalent to a ring homomorphism to its endomorphism ring over \(\mathbb{Z}\text{.}\) Given a representation \(\rho:G\to \operatorname{Aut}_R(V)\) by considering \(\operatorname{Aut}_R(V)\subseteq \operatorname{End}_\mathbb{Z}(V)\) we get a group homomorphism \(f\) to the unit subgroup of \(\operatorname{End}_\mathbb{Z}(V)\text{.}\) The action of \(R\) on \(V\) gives a ring homomorphism \(\iota: R \to \operatorname{End}_\mathbb{Z}(V)\text{.}\) For \(r\in R\) and \(g\in G\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} (f(g) \circ \iota(r))(v) = f(g)(rv) = \rho(g)(rv) = r \rho(g)(v) = (\iota(r) \circ f(g))(v) \end{equation*}
for all \(v\in V\text{.}\) Thus, by the universal property, we get a well-defined ring homomorphism \(R[G] \to \operatorname{End}_\mathbb{Z}(V)\text{,}\) and hence an \(R[G]\)-module structure, which is easily seen to follow the formula above.
We leave the final claim as an exercise.
