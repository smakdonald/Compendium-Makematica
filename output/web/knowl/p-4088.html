However, this polynomial also lives in \(F(a)\text{,}\) so we have \([F(a,b):F(a)]\) [[Mathematics/Definitions/Algebraic#$ defn$ â€“ Algebraic (Extension)|algebraic]]. As \([F(a):F]\) is also algebraic, we have \(F[a,b]\) algebraic as well, as it is a finite extension by the The Degree Formula. Thus \(a+b, a\inv,\) and \(ab\) are algebraic over \(F\) as well, making \(K\) a field.
