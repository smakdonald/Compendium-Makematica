Sylow theory gives that \(n_2=|\operatorname{Syl}_2(G)|\) is either \(1\) or \(3\) and \(n_3= |\operatorname{Syl}_3(G)|\) is either \(1\) or \(4\text{.}\) If either of these numbers is \(1\text{,}\) we have a unique subgroup of order \(4\) or of order \(3\text{,}\) and such a subgroup must be normal. Suppose these numbers are \(3\) and \(4\text{,}\) respectively. We deduce a contradition by “counting elements”.
