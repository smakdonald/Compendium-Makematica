Item 1.

The roots of \(f\) are the following:
  • \(\displaystyle \a_{1}=\sqrt[4]{7}e^{\frac{\pi i}{4}}=\sqrt[4]{7}(\cos(\frac{\pi}{4})+i\sin(\frac{\pi}{4}))=\sqrt[4]{7}\cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}(1+i)=\frac{\sqrt[4]{28}}{2}(1+i)\)
  • \(\displaystyle \a_{2}=\sqrt[4]{7}e^{\frac{3\pi i}{4}}=\sqrt[4]{7}(\cos(\frac{3\pi}{4})+i\sin(\frac{3\pi}{4}))=\sqrt[4]{7}\cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}(-1+i)=\frac{\sqrt[4]{28}}{2}(-1+i)\)
  • \(\displaystyle \a_{3}=\sqrt[4]{7}e^{\frac{5\pi i}{4}}=\sqrt[4]{7}(\cos(\frac{5\pi}{4})+i\sin(\frac{5\pi}{4}))=\sqrt[4]{7}\cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}(-1-i)=\frac{\sqrt[4]{28}}{2}(-1-i)\)
  • \(\displaystyle \a_{4}=\sqrt[4]{7}e^{\frac{7\pi i}{4}}=\sqrt[4]{7}(\cos(\frac{7\pi}{2})+i\sin(\frac{7\pi}{2}))=\sqrt[4]{7}\cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}(1-i)=\frac{\sqrt[4]{28}}{2}(1-i)\)
Notice that \(\a_1+\a_4=\sqrt[4]{28}\text{,}\) and thus \(\sqrt[4]{28}\in E\text{.}\)
