Next, consider the polynomial \(x^2+1\text{.}\) This has a root \(i\text{,}\) which is not in \(\Q(\sqrt[3]{2})\) as \(\Q(\sqrt[3]{2})\subseteq\R\) and \(i\not\in\R\text{.}\) Let \(L=\Q(\sqrt[3]{2})\) for convenience. As \(x^2+1\text{,}\) has degree 2, it is irreducible by Theorem 2.2 [provisional cross-reference: empty], making it the minimal polynomial of \(L(i)\text{.}\) Thus by Theorem 2.31 [provisional cross-reference: empty] we see that \([L(i):L]=2\text{.}\) Thus by the degree formula [provisional cross-reference: empty] we have \([L(i):\Q]=[L(i):L][L:\Q]=2(3)=6\text{.}\)
