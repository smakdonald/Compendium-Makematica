Let \(R\) be a ring (with \(1\)). A left \(R\)-module is an abelian group \((M,+)\) together with a pairing \(R \times M \to M\text{,}\) written \((r,m) \mapsto rm\text{,}\) such that for all \(r,s \in R\) and \(m,n \in M\)
  1. \((r + s)m = rm + sm\text{,}\)
  2. \((rs)m = r(sm)\text{,}\)
  3. \(r(m + n) = rm + rn\text{,}\) and
  4. \(1m = m\text{.}\)
