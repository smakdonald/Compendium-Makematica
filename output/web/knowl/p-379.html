Suppose \(G\) is a group and \(n\) is a positive integer. Then
  1. If \(H\leq G\) is a subgroup of order \(n\text{,}\) then \(gHg^{-1}\leq G\) is a subgroup of order \(n\text{.}\)
  2. If there is a unique subgroup \(H\) of \(G\) of order \(n\text{,}\) then \(H \unlhd G\text{.}\)
