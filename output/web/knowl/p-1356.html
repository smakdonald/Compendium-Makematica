  1. Given a representation \((V,\rho)\text{,}\) the map
    \begin{equation*} xy matrix \end{equation*}
    satisfies the properties
    1. \(\displaystyle e \cdot v= v\)
    2. \(\displaystyle gh \cdot v = g \cdot (h \cdot v)\)
    3. \(\displaystyle g \cdot (v+w)=(g \cdot v) + (g \cdot w)\)
    4. \(g \cdot rv = r (g\cdot v)\text{,}\)
    In particular, the first two conditions say that \(G\) acts on \(V\) in the sense of group action on a set, and the last two say that the action of any element is by an \(R\)-linear map. Conversely, any such function \(\psi\) yields a representation \((V,\rho)\text{.}\)
  2. If \(V=R^n\) is free, then \(\operatorname{Aut}_R(V) \cong \operatorname{GL}_n(R)\text{,}\) where \(\operatorname{GL}_n(R)\) is the group of \(n\times n\) invertible matrices with entries in \(R\text{.}\) By a slight abuse of notation, we will say that a group homomorphism \(G\to \operatorname{GL}_n(V)\) is a representation of \(G\text{.}\)
