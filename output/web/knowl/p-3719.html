Part (b) Let \(A\) be a nilpotent \(5\times 5\) matrix. There exists some \(m\in\N\) such that \(A^m=0\text{.}\) Let \(\lambda\) be an eigenvalue of \(A\text{,}\) and so \(\lambda^m\) is an eigenvalue for \(A^m=0\text{.}\) As \(F\) is a field and thus an integral domain, we see that \(\lambda^n=0\) implies that \(\lambda=0\) as well.
