Recall that the centralizer of a subgroup \(H\) in a group \(G\) is
\begin{equation*} C_G(H) = \{g ∈ G | gh = hg \text{ for all }h ∈ H\}. \end{equation*}
(a) Prove that if \(H\) is normal in \(G\text{,}\) then \(C_G(H)\) is normal in \(G\text{.}\) (b) Prove that if \(H\) is normal in \(G\text{,}\) then \(G/C_G(H)\) is isomorphic to a subgroup of \(\Aut(H)\) (the group of automorphisms of H)
