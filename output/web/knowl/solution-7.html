Solution 3.2.3.1.
Consider
\begin{equation*} V=\{e, (1 2)(3 4), (1 3)(2 4), (1 4)(2 3)\}. \end{equation*}
From Exercise 3.2.2 we have \(V \nsg S_4\text{.}\) The group \(V\) has order \(4\text{,}\) making it ableian from Exercise 8. Thus every subgroup of \(V\) is normal in \(V\) by Exercise 3.2.2.
Now consider the subgroup \(H = \{e,(12)(34)\}\text{.}\) But \(H\) is not normal in \(S_4\text{,}\) since for example
\begin{equation*} (1 3) [(1 2)(3 4)] (1 3)^{-1} = (3 2)(1 4) \not\in H. \end{equation*}
