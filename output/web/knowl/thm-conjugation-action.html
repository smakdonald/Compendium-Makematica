Theorem 4.7.

  1. Prove that the conjugation action of of \(G\) on itself is a well-defined group action.
  2. Show \(G\) acts on \(H\) via conjugation for any \(H\leq G\text{.}\)
  3. Let \(N\mathrel{\unlhd}G\text{.}\) The conjugation action of \(G\) on itself induces an action by conjugation of \(G\) on \(N\text{.}\) In particular, \(N\) is the disjoint union of some of the conjugacy classes in \(G\text{.}\)
