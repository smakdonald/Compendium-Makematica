Intuitively, a normal subgroup of a group is a subgroup that is invariant under conjugation by any element of the larger group. That is, if \(N\) is a normal subgroup of \(G\text{,}\) then for any element \(g\) in \(G\text{,}\) the conjugate of \(N\) by \(g\text{,}\) denoted by \(gNg\inv\text{,}\) is also a subgroup of \(G\) that is equal to \(N\text{.}\) Normal subgroups are important in group theory because they play a crucial role in the definition and study of quotient groups, which we will explore in SectionÂ 3.3.
