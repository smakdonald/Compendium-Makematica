\(\rem\).

Recall that when \(F\) is a field an \(F\)-module homomorphism is called a \(F\)-linear transformation. Since every \(F\)-vector space has a basis, every linear transformation between finite dimensional vector spaces may be represented by a matrix, as we noted before.
\(\defn\) – Matrix of Free Module Homomorphism.
Let \(R\) be a non-zero commutative ring and let \(V,W\text{,}\) be \(R\)-modules of finite rank \(n\) and \(m\text{,}\) respectively. (For instance, \(R\) could be a field and \(V\) and \(W\) could be arbitrary finite dimensional vector spaces.) Let \(B=\{b_1, \dots, b_n\}\) and \(C=\{c_1, \dots, c_m\}\) be ordered bases of \(V\) and \(W\text{.}\) 1. For each \(v \in V\text{,}\) let \([v]^B\) denote the unique column vector \(\begin{bmatrix} r_1 \\ \vdots \\ r_n \end{bmatrix}\) such that \(v = \sum_i r_i b_i\text{.}\) Define \([w]^C\) for \(w \in W\) similarly. 2. \(\exe\) – \(\R\)-Vector Space of Polynomials (\(P_n\)).
Let \(P_n\) denote the the \(\R\)-vector space of polynomials of degree at most \(n\) (including the zero polynomial) and consider the linear transformation \(\phi :P_3\to P_2\) given by \(\phi(f)=f'\text{,}\) i.e. taking the derivative. Take \(B=\{1,x,x^2,x^3\}\) for an ordered basis of \(P_3\) and \(C = \{1,x,x^2\}\) for an ordered basis of \(P_2\text{.}\) Then for \(p(x) = 5 + 3x + 2x^2 - 7x^3\) we have
\begin{equation*} [p(x)]^B = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ -7\end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} [\phi]_B^C= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 &0 & 0 \\ 0 &0 & 2 & 0 \\ 0& 0& 0& 3 \\ \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
We have
\begin{equation*} [\phi]_B^C \cdot [p(x)]^B = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \\ -21 \end{bmatrix} = [p'(x)]^C = [\phi(p(x))]^C \end{equation*}

\(\exe\) – Identity Automorphism of Free \(R\)-Module.
If \(\id_V: V \to V\) is the identity automorphism of an \(n\)-dimensional free \(R\)-module \(V\text{,}\) then for any basis \(B\) of \(V\) we have \(\id_V(b_i) = b_i\) for all \(i\) and hence[^1]
\begin{equation*} [\id_V]^B_B = I_n. \end{equation*}
\(\prop\) – When FMH Matrix is Invertible.
If \(f: V \to W\) is an isomorphism of free modules of finite rank, \(B\) is an ordered basis of \(V\) and \(C\) is an ordered basis of \(W\text{,}\) then \([f]_B^C\)[^1] is an invertible matrix and
\begin{equation*} [f^{-1}]_C^B = \left([f]_B^C\right)^{-1}. \end{equation*}
