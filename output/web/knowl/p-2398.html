We know that \(L=\Q(\sqrt[5]{11},\z)\text{,}\) as every generator of \(L\) can be written using \(\z\) and \(\sqrt[5]{11}\text{.}\) Let \([L:\Q]=n\text{,}\) \([L:F]=p\text{,}\) and \([L:E]=q\text{.}\) By the The Degree Formula, we have
  • \([L:\Q]=[L:F][F:\Q]\text{,}\) and thus \(n=4p\)
  • \([L:\Q]=[L:E][E:\Q]\text{,}\) and thus \(n=5q\)
As \(E\neq L\) and \(F\neq L\) we know that \(p,q>1\text{.}\)
