The assumption that \(f\) be monic in this Proposition is necessary. Consider for instance \(f(x) = (3x+1)(x+1) = 3 x^2 + 4x + 1 \in \Z[x]\text{.}\) Modding out by \(3\) yields a linear polynomial in \((\Z/3)[x]\) which is thus irreducible. But \(f\) isn't irreducible in \(\Z[x]\text{.}\) (The Proposition can be generalized to non-monic polynomials by adding the assumption that \(p\) does not divided the leading coefficient of \(f\text{.}\))
