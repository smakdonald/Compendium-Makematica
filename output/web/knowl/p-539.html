Recall from your undergraduate algebra course the construction for the integers modulo \(n\text{:}\) one starts with an equivalence relation \(\sim\) on \(\mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) considers the set \(\mathbb{Z}/n\) of all equivalence classes with respect to this equivalence relation, and verifies that the operations on \(\mathbb{Z}\) give rise to well defined binary operations on the set of equivalence classes.
