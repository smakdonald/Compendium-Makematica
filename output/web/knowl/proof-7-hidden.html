1. First, note that the identity element of \(S_n\) is the empty product of cycles.
    Fix \(\sigma\in S_n\) and let \(G = \langle \sigma\rangle\text{.}\) The homomorphism given as the inclusion of \(G\) into \(S_n\) determines an action of \(G\) on the set \(X := \{1, \dots, n\}\text{.}\) This action decomposes \(X\) into disjoint orbits. Since \(\sigma\) generates \(G\text{,}\) \(i, j\) belong to the same orbit iff \(\sigma^k(i) = j\) for some \(k \geq 0\text{.}\) Let \(O\) be any one of these orbits with size at least \(2\text{.}\) Now pick any element \(i \in O\text{.}\) Consider the sequence
    \begin{equation*} i, \sigma(i), \sigma^2(i), \dots \end{equation*}
    of element of \(O\text{.}\) The axioms of a group action gives us that the first time this sequence has a repitition occurs when \(\sigma^p(i_1) = i_1\text{,}\) where \(p = \#O\text{.}\) Let \(j_t =\sigma^{t+1}(i)\) for \(t = 1, \dots, p\) and let \(\tau_O\) be the \(p\)-cycle \((j_1 \, \dots \, j_p)\text{.}\) Then \(\sigma\) and \(\tau_O\) “act the same” on \(O\text{.}\) More formally,
    \begin{equation*} \sigma\circ \tau_O^{-1} \end{equation*}
    fixes all elements of \(O\text{.}\) Also, \(\tau_O\) fixes \(\{1, \dots, n\} \setminus O\text{.}\) It follows that
    \begin{equation*} \sigma= \prod_{O} \tau_O \end{equation*}
    where the product is taken over all orbits of size at least \(2\text{.}\) This proves existense.
    For uniqueness, if \(\sigma= \prod_i \gamma_i\) is a product of disjoint cycles, then the orbits \(X\) under the action of \(G\) are the one-element orbits along with the orbits of each \(\gamma_i\text{.}\) Moreover, if the orbit of \(\gamma_i\) is \(O\text{,}\) then \(\gamma_i\) and \(\sigma\) act the same on \(O\) and \(\gamma_i\) fixes \(\{1, \dots, n\} \setminus O\text{.}\) It follows that \(\gamma_i = \tau_O\text{.}\)
