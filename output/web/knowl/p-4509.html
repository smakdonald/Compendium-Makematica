Part (b) Let \(rN\) be a nilpotent element in \(R/N\text{.}\) Then \(r^nN=N\text{,}\) meaning \(r^n\in N\text{.}\) This means there exists some \(m\in\N\) such that \(r^{n^m}=r^{nm}=0\text{,}\) placing \(r\in N\text{.}\) Thus all nilpotent elements in \(R/N\) are actually \(0\) (possibly four \(0\)'s in a trench coat).
