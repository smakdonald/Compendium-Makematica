Problem G.163. Problem 9 (*).

Let \(A\) be a square matrix over the field \(\C\) of complex numbers.
  1. Suppose \(A\) is invertible. Prove that there is a square matrix \(B\) over \(\C\) such that \(B^2=A\text{.}\)
  2. Show by example that (1) can fail if \(A\) is not assumed to be invertible.
