We show that since \(R/M\) is a domain, \(M\) must be prime. Suppose by way of contradiction that \(M\) is not prime, so exist elements \(x,y\in R\) such that \(xy\in M\) but neither \(x\) nor \(y\) are in \(M\text{.}\) Thus \(xM\cdot yM=xyM=M\text{,}\) making \(x\) and \(y\) zerodivisors in \(R/M\text{,}\) contradicting the fact that \(R/M\) is a field. Thus \(M\) is prime.
