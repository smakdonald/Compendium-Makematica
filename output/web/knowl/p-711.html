If \(A\) is any subset of a ring \(R\text{,}\) the ideal generated by \(A\text{,}\) denoted \((A)\text{,}\) is the intersection of all ideals of \(R\) that contain \(A\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} (A)=\bigcap_{I\;\text{ideal of}\;R, A\subseteq I} I. \end{equation*}
An ideal \(I\) is finitely generated if \(I = (A)\) for some finite subset \(A\) of \(R\text{.}\)
