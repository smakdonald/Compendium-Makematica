Let \(G\) be a finite group. (a) Suppose every subgroup of \(G\) is normal. Prove that given any positive divisor \(d\) of \(|G|\) there exists a subgroup of \(G\) of order \(d\text{.}\) (b) Give an example, with justification, of a finite group \(G\) and a positive divisor \(d\) of \(|G|\) such that \(G\) has no subgroup of order \(d\text{.}\)
