Corollary 3.6.

If \(G\) is a finite group and \(H \leq G\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} |G| & = |H| \cdot (\text{the number of left cosets of $H$ in $G$}) \\ & = |H| \cdot (\text{the number of right cosets of $H$ in $G$}). \\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
In particular the number of left cosets of \(H\) in \(G\) is equal to the number of right cosets of \(H\) in \(G\text{.}\)
Furthermore, even if \(G\) is not finite the number of left and right cosets of a subgroup \(H\leq G\) is still the same. (Hint: consider the map \(gH\mapsto Hg^{-1}\) and show it's a bijection. Why is the inverse needed?)
