Because \(p(x)\) is irreducible and \(F[x]\) is a PID, \((p(x))\) is a maximal ideal [provisional cross-reference: empty]. Thus \(L = F[x]/(p(x))\) is a field [provisional cross-reference: empty]. The map \(F \to L\) given by \(a \mapsto \ov{a}\) is a ring map since it is the composition of the two ring maps \(F \xra{can} F[x] \xra{can} F[x]/(p(x))\) [provisional cross-reference: empty]. Since it is a ring map between two fields, it is injective [provisional cross-reference: empty].
