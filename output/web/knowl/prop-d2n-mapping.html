Proposition 3.32. \(D_{2n}\) Generators and Unique Homomorphism.

If \(G\) is a group and \(x,y \in G\) are elements such that \(x^n = e\text{,}\) \(y^2 = e\text{,}\) and \(yxy = x^{-1}\) for some integer \(n \geq 3\text{,}\) then there exists a unique group homomorphism \(f: D_{2n} \to G\) such that \(f(r) = x\) and \(f(s) = y\text{.}\)
