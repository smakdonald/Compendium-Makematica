Paragraph
  1. Images are Subgroups.
    If \(\varphi: G \to H\) is a homomorphism of groups, then the image of \(\varphi\) is a subgroup of \(H\text{.}\)
  2. Kernels are Subgroups.
    If \(\varphi: G \to H\) is a homomorphism of groups, then the kernel of \(\varphi\) is a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)
  3. Preimages are Subgroups.
    Prove that if \(\varphi:G\to H\) is a group homomorphism and \(K\leq H\) then the preimage of \(K\text{,}\) defined as \(\varphi^{-1}(K)=\{g\in G | \varphi(g)\in K\}\text{,}\) is a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)
