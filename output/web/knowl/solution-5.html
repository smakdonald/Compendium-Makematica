Solution 4.9.1.

Let \(G\) act on \(H\) by conjugation, yielding the homomorphism \(\rho\) via the permutation representation that maps \(g\) to \(\s_g\text{,}\) where \(\s_g(h)=ghg\inv\text{.}\) This is the trivial permutation exactly when \(g\in C_G(H)\text{,}\) making \(C_G(H)\) the kernel of \(\rho\text{.}\) Thus \(C_G(H)\) is normal in \(G\text{.}\)
From the above permutation representation we have a homomorphism \(\rho: G\to S_{|H|}\cong\Aut(H)\text{.}\) As \(C_G(H)=\ker(\rho)\text{,}\) by the First Isomorphism Theorem we have \(G/C_G(H)\cong\im(\rho))\text{,}\) a subgroup of \(\Aut(H)\text{.}\)
