Let \(G\) be a finitely generated abelian group. Then there exist integers \(r,s \geq 0\text{,}\) positive prime integers \(p_1, \ldots, p_s\text{,}\) and strictly positive integers \(e_i \geq 1\) such that
\begin{equation*} G \cong \Z^r\oplus\Z/p_1^{e_1} \oplus \dots \oplus \Z/p_s^{e_s}. \end{equation*}
Moreover, the \(r,p_i\)'s, and \(e_i\)'s are uniquely determined by \(G\) (up to ordering).
