\(\defn\) – Galois Extension.

A finite extension of fields is a \(\defnn{Galois extension}\) if the three equivalence conditions of this theorem hold: 1. \(|\Aut(L/F)| = [L:F]\)[^2] 2. The extension is both normal and separable. 3. \(L\) is the splitting field of some separable polynomial with coefficients in \(F\text{.}\)
In this case it is customary to write \(\Gal(L/F)\) for the group \(\Aut(L/F)\)[^1] and to refer to it as the \(\defnn{Galois group}\) of the extension – they are exactly the same group, but the former is used only when the extension is Galois.
