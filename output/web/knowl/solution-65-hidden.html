Let \(\alpha,\beta\in\Z[i]\) and behold \(\begin{align}N(\alpha\beta)&=N((a+bi)(c+di))\\&=N(ac-bd+adi+bci)\\&=(ac-bd)^2+(ad+bc)^2\\&=((ac)^2-2acbd+(bd)^2)+((ad)^2+2abcd+(bc^2))\\&=a^2c^2+b^2d^2+a^2d^2+b^2c^2\\&=(a^2c^2+a^2d^2)+(b^2c^2+b^2d^2)\\&=a^2(c^2+d^2)+b^2(c^2+d^2)\\N(\alpha)N(\beta)&=(a^2+b^2)(c^2+d^2).\end{align}\) So there's that done.
  2. Suppose \(\alpha\beta=1\text{.}\) Then by Part (a) we know
    \begin{equation*} N(\alpha)N(\beta)=N(\alpha\beta)=N(1)=1, \end{equation*}
    so \((a^2+b^2)(c^2+d^2)=1\text{,}\) with \(a,b,c,d\in\Z\text{.}\) Thus we have \(a^2+b^2=1=c^2+d^2\text{.}\) So the units of \(\Z[i]\) are \(\{\pm 1,\pm i\}\text{.}\)
