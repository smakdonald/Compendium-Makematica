Let \(R\) be a ring, \(M\) and \(N\) left \(R\)-modules, and \(p: M \to N\) an \(R\)-module homomorphism. Assume that \(p\) is surjective. We say \(p\) is a {} if there exists an \(R\)-module homomorphism \(j: N \to M\) such that \(p \circ j = id_N\) (i.e., \(p(j(n)) = n\) for all \(n \in N\)).
  1. Prove that if \(N\) is free, then every surjective \(R\)-module homomorphism of the form \(p: M \to N\) is a split surjection.
  2. 2. By giving an explicit example with justification, show the statement in part (1) would become false if \(N\) were not assumed to be free.
