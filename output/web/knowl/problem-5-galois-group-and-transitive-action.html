Problem 5 – Galois Group and Transitive Action.

Let \(E/F\) be a finite Galois extension and let \(G\) be the Galois group of \(E/F\text{.}\)Suppose that \(E = F (\a)\) and let \(f(x)\) be the minimum polynomial of \(\a\) over \(F\) . Prove that
\begin{equation*} f(x) =\prod_{\s\in G} (x-\s(\a)). \end{equation*}
###### Proof. Let \(E/F\) be a finite Galois extension and let \(G\) be the Galois group of \(E/F\text{.}\) Suppose that \(E = F (\a)\) and let \(f(x)\) be the minimal polynomial of \(\a\) over \(F\text{.}\) Thus \(G\) acts on the roots of \(f\) faithfully.[^1] Additionally, as \(f\) is the minimal polynomial of \(\a\) it is irreducible, making the action transitive as well.
As \(E\) is Galois over \(F\) we know that \(f\) splits into linear factors, each of the form \(x-\b\text{,}\) where \(\b\) is a root of \(f\text{.}\) As our action is transitive, for every root \(\b\) there exists a \(\s\in G\) such that \(\s\cdot\a=b\text{,}\) or \(\s(\a)=\b\text{.}\) #### Problem 4 – Prime Degree and Splitting Field Let \(F\) be a field of characteristic \(p > 0\text{,}\) \(a \in F\) , and consider the polynomial \(f(x)=x^p -x-a \in F[x]\)[^1].

  2. Suppose \(f(x)\) is irreducible over \(F\) and let \(L\) be a splitting field of \(f\) over \(F\text{.}\) Prove that the Galois group of \(L\) over \(F\) is cyclic.
