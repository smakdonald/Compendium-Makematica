Solution G.73.1.

Let \(K\) be a Galois extension of \(F\) with \(|\Gal(K/F )| = 20\text{.}\)
Part (a) Let \(P\) be a Sylow \(2\)-subgroup of \(\Gal(K/F)\text{.}\) Notice that \(|P|=4\text{.}\) By the [[Mathematics/Modern Algebra/Results/Theorem – Fundamental Theorem of Galois Theory|FTGT]] there exists an intermediate field extension \(E\) such that \(|\Gal(K/E)|=4\text{.}\) By the [[Mathematics/Modern Algebra/Results/Proposition – The Degree Formula|Degree Formula]], we have \([K:F]=[K:E][E:F]\text{,}\) with \([K:F]=20\) and \([K:L]=4\text{.}\) Thus \([L:F]=5\text{.}\)
Part (b) Notice that as \(2\) is a prime dividing the order of \(\Gal(K/F )\) there must exist an element of order \(2\) by [[Mathematics/Modern Algebra/Results/Theorem – Cauchy's Theorem|Cauchy's Theorem]]. The cyclic subgroup, \(H\text{,}\) generated by this element has order \(2\text{.}\) By the FTGT there exists an intermediate field extension \(L\) such that \(|\Gal(K/L)|=2\text{.}\) By the Degree Formula, we have \([K:F]=[K:L][L:F]\text{,}\) with \([K:F]=20\) and \([K:L]=2\text{.}\) Thus \([L:F]=2\text{.}\)
