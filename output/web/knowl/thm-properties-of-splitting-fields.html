Theorem 1.66.

Let \(F\) be a field and \(f(x) \in F[x]\) a non-constant polynomial. 1. There exists a splitting field \(L\) for \(f(x)\) over \(F\text{.}\) 2. If \(F \subseteq L'\) is another splitting field of \(f(x)\) over \(F\text{,}\) then there is a field isomorphism \(\s: L \xra{\cong} L'\) such that \(\s|_F = \id_F\text{.}\) 3. The degree of any splitting field of \(f(x)\) is at most \(n!\) where \(n = \deg(f)\text{.}\)

Proof.
