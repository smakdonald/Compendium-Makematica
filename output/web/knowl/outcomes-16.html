Summary 5.2.

  • A Simple Group is a group with only two normal subgroups: itself and the trivial subgroup.
  • We have three main strategies to prove that a group is simple: by counting elements, acting on a set of Sylow \(p\)-subgroups, or by resorting to diving into the minutae with normalizers.  1 
  • \(A_5\) is simple.
