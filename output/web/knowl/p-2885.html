If \(F \subseteq L\) is a field extension and \(A\) is any subset of \(L\text{,}\) we write \(F(A)\) for the smallest subfield of \(L\) that contains all of \(F\) and \(A\) and it is called the subfield generated by \(A\) over \(F\text{.}\) Since the intersection of any two subfields of \(L\) is again a subfield, \(F(A)\) exists and is
\begin{equation*} F(A) = \bigcap_{\text{$E$ is a subfield of $L$ such that $E \supseteq F \cup A$}} E. \end{equation*}
where \(E\) fix Nearly always \(A\) will be a finite set, \(A = \{a_1, \dots, a_n\}\text{,}\) and we write \(F(a_1, \dots, a_n)\) for \(F(A)\text{.}\)
