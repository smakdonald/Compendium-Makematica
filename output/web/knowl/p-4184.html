Let \(L\) be the splitting field of the polynomial \(f(x)=x^7-18\) over \(\Q\text{.}\) First, note that \(f\) is irreducible in \(\Q[x]\) by Eisenstein's Criterion (\(p=2\)). The roots of \(f\) are the following: - \(a_1=\sqrt[7]{18}e^{\frac{2\pi i}{7}}\text{,}\) - \(a_2=\sqrt[7]{18}e^{\frac{4\pi i}{7}}\text{,}\) - \(a_3=\sqrt[7]{18}e^{\frac{6\pi i}{7}}\text{,}\) - \(a_4=\sqrt[7]{18}e^{\frac{8\pi i}{7}}\text{,}\) - \(a_5=\sqrt[7]{18}e^{\frac{10\pi i}{7}}\text{,}\) - \(a_6=\sqrt[7]{18}e^{\frac{12\pi i}{7}}\text{,}\) and - \(a_7=\sqrt[7]{18}e^{\frac{14\pi i}{7}}=\sqrt[7]{18}\text{.}\) Let \(\z=e^{\frac{2\pi i}{7}}\text{,}\) which is the root of the \(7\th\) cyclotomic polynomial (which has degree \(6,\) and thus irreducible in \(\Q[x]\text{.}\) Let \(F=\Q(\z)\) and \(E=\Q(\sqrt[7]{18})\text{.}\) Thus we have \([F:\Q]=6\) and \([E:\Q]=7\text{.}\) By the \(\gcd\) irreducible argument we see that \([L:\Q]=42\text{.}\) Yeesh.
