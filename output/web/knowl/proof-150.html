Proof

Say \(N \subseteq M\) is a submodule. By the claim marked (i) in the proof of Theorem [cross-reference to target(s) "prop93b" missing or not unique] every submodule of \(N\) is a summand, and hence \(N\) is semisimple by Theorem [cross-reference to target(s) "prop93b" missing or not unique] \((2)\Rightarrow (1)\text{.}\)
Given a surjection \(M \twoheadrightarrow P\text{,}\) it splits by Theorem [cross-reference to target(s) "prop93b" missing or not unique], so that \(P\) is isomorphic to a submodule of \(M\text{,}\) namely the image of \(P\) under the splitting map. Hence \(P\) is semisimple by the case already proven.
