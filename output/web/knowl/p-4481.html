Let \(G\) act on \(\Syl_7(G)\) by conjugation, yielding the homomorphism
\begin{equation*} \rho: G \to S_8 \end{equation*}
via the [[Proposition – Permutation Representation|permutation representation]]. This map is non-trivial from part (2) of Sylow's Theorem, but \(2^5\cdot 7^3\) does not divide \(|S_8|=8!\text{,}\) and thus \(\rho\) cannot be injective. Then the kernel of this homomorphism is non-trivial, normal subgroup of \(G\) (See: [[Corollary – Normal iff Kernel of Homomorphism|Result]]). Thus \(G\) is not simple.
