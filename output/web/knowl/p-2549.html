Given a field extension \(F \subseteq L\) and an element \(\a \in L\text{,}\) set
\begin{equation*} F[\a] = \{f(\a) \mid f(x) \in F[x]\}. \end{equation*}
Then \(F[\a]\) is a subring of \(L\) and in fact it is the smallest subring of \(L\) that contains \(F\) and \(\a.\) We define \(F(\a)\) to be the field of fractions of \(F[\a]\text{;}\) that is,
\begin{equation*} F(\a) = \left\{\frac{f(\a)}{g(\a)} \mid f(x), g(x) \in F[x] \text{ such that $g(\a) \ne 0$} \right\}. \end{equation*}
So, \(F(\a)\) is a subfield of \(L\) and it is the smallest subfield of \(L\) that contains \(F\) and \(\a\text{.}\)
