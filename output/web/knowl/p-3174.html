First, note that \(f\) is irreducible in \(\Q[x]\) by Eisenstein's Criterion (\(p=3\)) The roots of \(f\) are - \(\sqrt[4]3\text{,}\) - \(\sqrt[4]3i\text{,}\) - \(-\sqrt[4]3\text{,}\) and - \(-\sqrt[4]3i\text{.}\) Let \(F=\Q(\sqrt[4]3)\) and notice \([F:\Q]=4\text{.}\) As \(F\subseteq\R\) we see that \([F(i):F]=2\text{,}\) and thus \([K:\Q]=8\text{,}\) meaning our basis will have eight elements:
\begin{equation*} \{1, \sqrt[4]3, (\sqrt[4]3)^2, (\sqrt[4]3)^3,\sqrt[4]3i,(\sqrt[4]3)^2i, (\sqrt[4]3)^3i \} \end{equation*}
