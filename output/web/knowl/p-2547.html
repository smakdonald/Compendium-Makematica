Note that \(i\) is the root of \(x^2+1\text{,}\) and since \(F\subseteq\R\) and \(i\not\in\R\text{,}\) we see that \(x^2+1\) is irreducible in \(F\) due to it having degree 2. As \(x^2+1\) is monic and degree 2 we see its the minimal polynomial of \([F(i):F]=2\text{.}\) By the Degree Formula, we see that \([F(i):\Q]=[F(i):F][F:\Q]=(2)(4)=8\text{.}\)
