Solution 6.30.1.

By Sylow's Theorem we know the following: - \(n_3|5\) and \(n_3\equiv 1\mod{3}\text{,}\) so \(n_3=1\text{.}\) - \(n_5|\) and \(n_5\equiv1\mod{5}\text{,}\) so \(n_5=1\) as well. Thus there is exactly one Sylow \(5\)-subgroup, \(P\text{,}\) and exactly one Sylow \(3\)-subgroup, \(Q\text{.}\) Both are normal in \(G\text{.}\) Notice that \(Q\) has order \(9\text{,}\) a prime squared. Thus \(Q\) is abelian. By the FTFGAG, \(Q\) is either isomorphic to \(\Z_3\times\Z_3\) or \(\Z_9\text{.}\) Thus \(G\cong\Z_5\times\Z_9\) or \(G\cong\Z_5\times\Z_3\times\Z_3\text{.}\)
