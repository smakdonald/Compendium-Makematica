Let \(F\) be a field, \(V\) an \(F\)-vector space, and \(W\) a subspace of \(V\text{.}\) A subspace \(U\) of \(V\) is called a {} of \(W\) in \(V\) if \(U \cap W = \{0\}\) and \(U + W = V\text{.}\)
  1. Prove that for every \(V\) and \(W\) as above, \(W\) has at least one complement in \(V\text{.}\)
  2. Prove that if \(U\) is a complement of \(W\) in \(V\) and \(V\) is finite dimensional, then \(\dim_F(V)=\dim_F(W)+\dim_F(U)\text{.}\)
  3. Prove that if \(T\) is a subspace of \(V\) with \(V\) finite dimensional and \(\dim_F(T) + \dim_F(W) > \dim_F(V)\text{,}\) then \(T \cap W\) is non-zero.
