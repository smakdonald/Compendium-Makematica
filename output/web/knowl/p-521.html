Let \(H\) be a subgroup of a group \(G\text{.}\) Recall that the centralizer of \(H\) is the subgroup of \(G\) defined by \(Z_G(H) = \{g \in G | gh = hg ∀h \in H\}\)  2  and the normalizer of \(H\) is the subgroup of \(G\) defined by \(N_G(H) = \{g \in G | gHg\inv = H\}\text{.}\) Show that the centralizer \(C_G(H)\) of \(H\) in \(G\) is a normal subgroup of the normalizer \(N_G(H)\) of \(H\) in \(G\text{.}\)
