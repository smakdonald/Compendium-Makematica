Exercise Group 3.2.5–9. Qualifying Exam Problems.

5. January 2019 Problem (1).
Let \(G\) be a group. A subgroup \(H\) of \(G\) is called a characteristic subgroup of \(G\) if \(\a(H)=H\) for every automorphism \(\a\) of \(G\text{.}\) Show that if \(H\) is a characteristic subgroup of \(N\) and \(N\) is a normal subgroup of \(G\text{,}\) then \(H\) is a normal subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)
Solution.
6. May 2019 Problem (2a).
Let \(H\) be a subgroup of a group \(G\text{.}\) Recall that the centralizer of \(H\) is the subgroup of \(G\) defined by \(Z_G(H) = \{g \in G | gh = hg ∀h \in H\}\)  1  and the normalizer of \(H\) is the subgroup of \(G\) defined by \(N_G(H) = \{g \in G | gHg\inv = H\}\text{.}\) Show that the centralizer \(C_G(H)\) of \(H\) in \(G\) is a normal subgroup of the normalizer \(N_G(H)\) of \(H\) in \(G\text{.}\)
7. May 2021 Problem (1ab).
Let \(G\) be a group (not necessarily finite) and \(H\) a nonempty subset of \(G\) that is closed under multiplication. Suppose that for all \(g\in G\) we have \(g^2\in H\text{.}\) Prove the following:
  1. \(H\) is a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)
  2. \(H\) is normal.

  2. Suppose now that \(H\nsg G\) and \([G:H]=p\text{,}\) where \(p\) is prime. Prove that either \(K\leq H\) or \(G=KH\)
