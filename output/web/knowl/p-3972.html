Part (b) Suppose \(Z(G)\) is trivial, and let \(G\) act on \(S\) by conjugation. Thus the orbits of this action correspond the normalizers of elements and the stabilizers correspond to the centralizers. Recall that the orbits of this action partition \(S\text{,}\) so we have
\begin{equation*} |S|=\sum\limits_{x\in S}|\Orb_G(x)| \end{equation*}
for distinct orbits. However, by Orbit-Stabilizer Theorem we also have \(|G|=|\Orb(x)|\cdot |\Stab(x)|\) for all \(x\in S\text{.}\) Now recall the Class Equation:
\begin{equation*} |G|=|Z(G)|+\sum\limits_{g\in G}[G:C_G(g)] \end{equation*}
With all that out of the way, let \(x,y\in S\text{.}\) Notice that \(x^{p^{m}}=e=y^{p^{m}}\)
