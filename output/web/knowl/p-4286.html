Suppose \(A\) and \(B\) are subfields of a field extension \(K/F\) with \([A : F ]\) and \([B : F ]\) both finite. Let \(E\) be the subfield of K generated by \(A\) and \(B\text{.}\) (a) Show that \([E : F ] \leq [A : F ][B : F ]\) (b) Prove that equality holds when \([A : F ]\) and \([B : F ]\) are relatively prime. (c) Prove there are two subfields of \(\R/\Q\text{,}\) \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) neither contained in the other, so that the inequality in part (a) is strict.
