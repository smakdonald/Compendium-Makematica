By Proposition, \([\id_V]_B^{B'}\) is an invertible matrix and its inverse
\begin{equation*} \begin{equation} \left([\id_V]_B^{B'}\right)^{-1}=[\id_V]_{B'}^B. \end{equation} \end{equation*}
It follows from that
\begin{equation*} [\id_V]_B^{B'} \cdot [v]^B = [v]^{B'}; \end{equation*}
that is, multiplication by \([\id_V]_B^{B'}\) really does change the representation of vectors by column vectors from one basis to another.
