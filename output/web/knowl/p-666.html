  1. Physics: group actions are used to study the symmetries of physical systems. For example, the group of rotations in three dimensions acts on the space of physical states of a rigid body. By studying this group action, we can understand the behavior of rigid bodies under rotations.
  2. Chemistry: group actions are used to study the symmetries of molecules. The group of symmetries of a molecule acts on the set of its atoms, and this group action can be used to understand the molecular structure and properties, such as its optical activity and reactivity.
  3. Computer Science: group actions are used in algorithms for encryption and data compression. For example, the group action of a permutation group on a set of data can be used to shuffle and encrypt the data. Similarly, the group action of a linear group on a vector space can be used to compress and store data.
