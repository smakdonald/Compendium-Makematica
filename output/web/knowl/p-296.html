The symmetric group \(S_n\) acts on a free \(R\)-module with basis \(b_1,\dots,b_n\) by permuting coordinates: \(\rho(\sigma)(b_i) = b_{\sigma(i)}\text{.}\) For a concrete example, \(S_3\) acts on \({\mathbb{R}}^3\text{,}\) where, for example \((132) \cdot (a_1,a_2,a_3) = (a_2,a_3,a_1)\text{.}\)
