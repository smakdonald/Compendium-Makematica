Theorem 4.48. UMP for Quotients.

Let \(G\) be a group and \(N\) a normal subgroup. If \(\varphi: G \to H\) is a homomorphism of groups such that \(N \subseteq \operatorname{ker}(\varphi)\text{,}\) then
  1. there exists a unique group homomorphism \(\overline{\varphi}: G/N \to H\) such that the composition of \(\overline{\varphi}\) and the quotient map \({\pi}: G\twoheadrightarrow G/N\) is \(\varphi\text{.}\)
  2. If \(\varphi\) is onto, then \(\overline{\varphi}\) is onto.
  3. Moreover, \(\operatorname{ker}(\overline{\varphi}) = \operatorname{ker}(\varphi)/N = \{ gN \mid \varphi(g) = e_H\}.\)

Proof.
