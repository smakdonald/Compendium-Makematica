Item 2.3.6.b.
Notice that as \(2\) is a prime dividing the order of \(\Gal(K/F )\) there must exist an element of order \(2\) by Cauchy's Theorem. The cyclic subgroup, \(H\text{,}\) generated by this element has order \(2\text{.}\) By the FTGT there exists an intermediate field extension \(L\) such that \(|\Gal(K/L)|=2\text{.}\) By the Degree Formula, we have \([K:F]=[K:L][L:F]\text{,}\) with \([K:F]=20\) and \([K:L]=2\text{.}\) Thus \([L:F]=2\text{.}\)
