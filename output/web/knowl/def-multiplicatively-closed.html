Definition 1.1. Multiplicately Closed Set.

Suppose \(R\) is a commutative ring and \(S \subseteq R\) is a subset such that
  1. \(1 \in S\text{,}\)
  2. \(S\) is closed under multiplication (i.e., if \(x,y \in S\text{,}\) then \(xy \in S\)), and
  3. \(S\) does not contain \(0\) nor any zerodivisors.
Such a subset \(S\) is called a multiplicatively closed subset of non zerodivisors of \(R\text{.}\)
