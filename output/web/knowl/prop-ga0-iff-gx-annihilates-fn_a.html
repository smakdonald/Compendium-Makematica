Proposition 14.23. \(g(A)=0\) iff \(g(x)\) Annihilates \(F^n_A\).

Given an \(n \times n\) matrix \(A\) and polynomial \(g(x)\in F[x]\text{,}\) we have \(g(A) = 0\) if and only if \(g(x)\) annihilates the \(F[x]\)-module \(F^n_A\text{.}\)
In particular, \(\mp_A(x)\) is the unique monic generator of the annihilator ideal
\begin{equation*} n_{F[x]}(F^n_A) := \{ g(x) \in F[x] \mid g(x) \cdot v = 0 \text{ for all $v \in F^n_A$}\}. \end{equation*}
