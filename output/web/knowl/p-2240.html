Let \(R\) be a commutative ring with \(1 \neq 0\text{.}\) An elementary row (column) operation on a matrix \(A \in \Mat_{m,n}(R)\) is one of the following three types of operations:
  1. (Type I) Adding an element of \(R\) times a row (column) of \(A\) to a different row column of \(A\text{.}\)
  2. (Type II) Multiplying a row (column) of \(A\) by a unit of \(R\text{.}\)
  3. (Type III) Interchanging two rows (columns) of \(A\text{.}\)
