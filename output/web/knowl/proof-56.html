Proof

For each \(1 \leq i \leq n\text{,}\) let \(I_i\) denote the subset of \(R\) consisting of matrices whose only nonzero entires belong to the \(i\)-th column. The rules for matrix addition and multiplication show that \(I_i\) is a left ideal (i.e., a left submodule) of \(R\text{.}\) Moreover, there is evident bijection between \(I_i\) and \(D^n\) (column vectors) and this bijection is an isomorphism of left \(R\)-modules. We proved \(D^n\) is simple as an \(R\)-module and hence so is \(I_i\text{.}\) Finally, \(R\) is the internal direct sum of \(I_1, \dots, I_n\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} R=I_1\oplus \cdots \oplus I_n \end{equation*}
because each matrix \(X\) is uniquely a sum of the form \(X_1 + \cdots + X_n\) with \(X_i \in M_i\text{.}\)
