Theorem 6.9. Rational Canonical Form.

Given a finite dimensional \(F\)-vector space \(V\) and an \(F\)-linear operator \(g: V \to V\text{,}\) there is a basis \(B\) of \(V\) such that the matrix representing \(g\) relative to \(B\) is
\begin{equation*} [g]_B^B = C(f_1) \oplus \cdots \oplus C(f_s) \end{equation*}
for monic polynomials \(f_1, \dots, f_s\) of degree at least one such that \(f_1 | f_2 | \cdots | f_s\text{.}\) Moreover, this matrix is unique, and is known as the rational canonical form of the operator \(g\text{.}\)
