Let \(R, A\) be rings and \(G\) a group. Given a ring homomorphism \(\iota: R \to A\) and a group homomorphism \(f: G \to (A^\times, \cdot)\text{,}\) such that for every \(r\in R, g\in G\) we have that \(\iota(r)\) and \(f(g)\) commute in \((A,\cdot)\text{,}\) there is a unique ring homomorphism \(\alpha: R[G] \to A\) such that \(\alpha|_R = \iota\) and \(\alpha|_G = f\text{.}\) Explicitly, \(\alpha\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \alpha\left(\sum_g r_g g\right) = \sum_g \iota(r_g) f(g). \end{equation*}
