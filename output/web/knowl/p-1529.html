We have
\begin{equation*} \mathbb{C}[G] \cong \operatorname{Mat}_{n_1}(\mathbb{C}) \times \cdots \times \operatorname{Mat}_{n_m}(\mathbb{C}) \end{equation*}
and \(m\) is the number of irreducible complex representations up to isomorphism. A key point is that the center of the right side is \(\mathbb{C}I_{n_1} \times \cdots \times \mathbb{C}I_{n_m}\text{,}\) which has dimension \(m\) as a complex vector space. Since this ring isomorphism is \(\mathbb{C}\)-linear, it induces a \(\mathbb{C}\)-linear isomorphism of the centers, and thus we just need to show that \(\dim_\mathbb{C}(Z(\mathbb{C}[G]))\) is equal to the number of conjugacy classes.
