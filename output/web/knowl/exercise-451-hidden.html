Let \(F\) be a field and recall that a square matrix \(A\) with entries in \(F\) is called [[Mathematics/Definitions/Nilpotent|nilpotent]] if \(A^m = 0\) for some positive integer \(m\text{.}\)
  1. Prove that if \(A\) is an \(n \times n\) nilpotent matrix, then \(A^n = 0\text{.}\)
  2. Assume \(n \leq 3\) and prove that two \(n \times n\) nilpotent matrices are similar if and only if they have the same [[Mathematics/Definitions/Rank|rank]]. (Recall the rank of a matrix is the [[Mathematics/Definitions/Dimension (Vector Space)|dimension]] of the vector space spanned by its columns.)
  3. Give an example, with justification, of two \(4 \times 4\) nilpotent matrices that have the same rank but are not similar.
