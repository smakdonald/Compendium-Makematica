It is customary to use cycle notation for permutations. If \(i_1, \dots, i_m\) are distinct integers between \(1\) and \(n\text{,}\) then \((i_1 \, i_2 \, \dots, i_m)\) denote the element of \(S_n\) that send \(i_1\) to \(i_2\text{,}\) \(i_2\) to \(i_3, \dots\text{,}\) \(i_{m-1}\) to \(i_m\) and \(i_m\) to \(i_1\text{,}\) and which fixes all elements of \(\{1, \dots, n\} \setminus \{i_1, \dots, i_m\}\text{.}\)
