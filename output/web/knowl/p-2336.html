Here is a concrete example. Let \(R = \R\text{,}\) \(V\) the collection of polynomials in \(x\) with coefficients in \(\R\) of degree at most \(m\text{.}\) Let \(D: V \to V\) be the map sending a polynomial to its derivative. The most obvious choice for a bases of \(V\) is \(1, x, \dots, x^m\text{.}\) With respect to this basis, the map \(D\) is represented by the \(n \times n\) matrix
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 3 & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \ddots & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & m \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
