  1. If \(\varphi: G \to H\) is a homomorphism of groups, then the image of \(\varphi\) is a subgroup of \(H\text{.}\)
  2. If \(\varphi: G \to H\) is a homomorphism of groups, then the kernel of \(\varphi\) is a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)
  3. Prove that if \(\varphi:G\to H\) is a group homomorphism and \(K\leq H\) then the preimage of \(K\text{,}\) defined as \(\varphi^{-1}(K)=\{g\in G | \varphi(g)\in K\}\text{,}\) is a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)
  4. If \(\varphi:G\to H\) and \(\phi:H\to K\) are group homomorphisms, the composition \(\phi\circ\varphi:G\to K\) is a group homomorphism.
  5. A group homomorphism \(\varphi: G \to H\) is injective if and only if \(\operatorname{ker}(\varphi) = \{e_G\}\text{.}\)
  6. A group homomorphism \(\varphi: G \to H\) is surjective if and only if \(\operatorname{im}(\varphi) = H\text{.}\)
