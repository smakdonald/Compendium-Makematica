The claim is equivalent to showing \(R\) is left semisimple if and only \(R^\mathrm{op}\) is, which in turn follows from just one of the implications. If \(R\) is left semisimple, then \(R\cong \prod_{i} \operatorname{Mat}_{n_i}(D_i)\text{,}\) so \(R^{\mathrm{op}} \cong \prod_i \operatorname{Mat}_{n_i}(D_i)^{\mathrm{op}} \cong \prod_i \operatorname{Mat}_{n_i}(D_i^{\mathrm{op}})\) so \(R^{\mathrm{op}}\) is left semisimple.
