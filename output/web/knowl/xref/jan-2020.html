1. Problem 1.

(1) Let \(G\) be a finite group.
(a) [12 pts] Suppose every subgroup of \(G\) is normal. Prove that given any positive divisor \(d\) of \(|G|\) there exists a subgroup of \(G\) of order \(d\text{.}\)
(b) [8 pts] Give an example, with justification, of a finite group \(G\) and a positive divisor \(d\) of \(|G|\) such that \(G\) has no subgroup of order \(d\text{.}\)
Solution.

2. Problem 2.

(2) Let \(G\) be a group of order \(p^{n}\text{,}\) for some prime \(p\text{,}\) acting on a finite set \(X\text{.}\)
(a) [10 pts] Suppose \(p\) does not divide \(|X|\text{.}\) Prove that there exists some element of \(X\) fixed by all elements of \(G\text{.}\)
(b) \([\mathbf{1 0} \mathbf{~ p t s}]\) Suppose \(G\) acts faithfully on \(X\text{.}\) Prove that \(|X| \geq n p\text{.}\)
Solution.

8. Problem 8.

(8) Let \(n\) be a positive integer and let \(p\) be a prime integer. Consider the polynomial
\begin{equation*} q(x)=x^{p^{n}}-x \in(\mathbb{Z} / p)[x] \end{equation*}
and define \(K\) to be the splitting field of \(q\) over \(\mathbb{Z} / p\text{.}\)
(a) [10 points] Show that the set \(E\) of roots of \(q(x)\) in \(K\) is a subfield of \(K\text{.}\)
(b) [10 points] Show that \(K\) has exactly \(p^{n}\) elements.
Solution.
