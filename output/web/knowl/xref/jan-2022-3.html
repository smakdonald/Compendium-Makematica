Exercise 3. Problem 3.

  1. Let \(K\) be a subgroup of a group \(G\text{.}\) Prove that \(K \unlhd G\) if and only if there is a group \(H\) and a homomorphism \(\phi: G \rightarrow H\) such that \(K=\operatorname{ker}(\phi)\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(G\) be the group of all \(2 \times 2\) matrices with entries from \(\mathbb{Z}\) having determinant 1 . Let \(p\) be a prime number, and take \(K\) to be the subset of \(G\) consisting of all \(\left[\begin{array}{ll}a & b \\ c & d\end{array}\right]\) with \(a \equiv d \equiv 1\) \((\bmod p)\) and \(b \equiv c \equiv 0(\bmod p)\text{.}\) Prove that \(K\) is a normal subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)
