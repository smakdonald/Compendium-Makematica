Theorem 8.24. Domains and Fields.

  1. Every field is an integral domain.
  2. A finite integral domain \(R\) must be a field.

Proof.

  1. Let \(F\) be a field. By Theorem 8.11 we know that every nonzero element of \(F\) is a unit. Thus \(F\) has no zerodivisors by Lemma 8.23.
  2. Let \(x\in R\text{,}\) and consider the set \(\{x^n|n\in\N\}\text{.}\) As \(R\) is finite there must exist \(n<m\in\N\) such that \(x^n=x^m\text{.}\) Since \(R\) is an integral domain Lemma 8.22 allows us to cancel an \(x^n\) from both sides, yielding \(e=x^{m-n}\text{,}\) and thus \(x\cdot x^{m-n-1}=e\text{,}\) making \(x\) a unit of \(R\text{.}\) Thus \(R\) is a field by Theorem 8.11.
