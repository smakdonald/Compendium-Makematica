Definition 2.1. Generated Subgroup.

Given a group \(G\) and a subset \(A\) of \(G\text{,}\) the subgroup of \(G\) generated by \(A\), written \(\langle A \rangle\text{,}\) is the smallest subgroup of \(G\) containing \(A\text{.}\) In symbols,
\begin{equation*} \langle A \rangle := \bigcap_{H \leq G, H \supseteq X} H. \end{equation*}
The elements of \(A\) are known as generators, and \(A\) is called a generating set.
