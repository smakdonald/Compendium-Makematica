Theorem 1.84. Properties of Subgroups.

  1. Transitivity of Subgroups.
    If \(H\) is a subgroup of \(G\) and \(K\) is a subgroup of \(H\text{,}\) then \(K\) is a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)
  2. Intersections of Subgroups.
    If \(H_\alpha\) is a subgroup of \(G\) for all \(\alpha\) in an index set \(J\text{,}\) then \(H=\bigcap_{\alpha\in J} H_\alpha\) is a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)
  3. Unions of Subgroups.
    Let \(G\) be a group and \(H\text{,}\) \(H'\) subgroups. Prove that \(H\cup H'\) is a subgroup if and only if \(H\subseteq H'\) or \(H'\subseteq H\text{.}\)
