1. Problem 1.

Let \(G\) be a finite group and let \(H\) be a subgroup of \(G\) of index \(p\text{,}\) where \(p\) is the smallest prime divisor of the order of \(G\text{.}\) Prove that \(H\) is normal in \(G\text{.}\)
Solution.

2. Problem 2.

Let \(G\) be a group of order \(385=5 \cdot 7 \cdot 11\text{.}\) Prove that \(G\) has a normal subgroup of order 11 and that the center of \(G\) contains a subgroup of order 7.
Solution.

3. Problem 3.

Let \(G\) be a group and \(H\) a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) Recall that the centralizer of \(H\) in \(G\) is
\begin{equation*} C_{H}(G)=\{g \in G \mid g h=h g \text { for all } h \in H\} \text {. } \end{equation*}
Prove that if \(H\) is normal in \(G\text{,}\) then so is \(C_{G}(H)\) and that \(G / C_{G}(H)\) is isomorphic to a subgroup of the automorphism group of \(H\text{.}\)
Solution.

4. Problem 4.

Let \(L \subseteq F \subseteq E\) be fields.
  1. Prove that \(E\) is algebraic over \(L\) if and only if \(E\) is algebraic over \(F\) and \(F\) is algebraic over \(L\text{.}\)
  2. Give an example where \(E / F\) and \(F / L\) are finite Galois extensions but \(E / L\) is not Galois.
Solution.

5. Problem 5.

Let \(E / F\) be a finite Galois extension and let \(G\) be the Galois group of \(E / F\text{.}\) Suppose that \(E=F(\alpha)\) and let \(f(x)\) be the minimal polynomial of \(\alpha\) over \(F\text{.}\) Prove that
\begin{equation*} f(x)=\prod_{\sigma \in G}(x-\sigma(\alpha)) \end{equation*}
Solution.

6. Problem 6.
