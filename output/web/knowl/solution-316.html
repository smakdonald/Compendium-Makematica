Solution G.111.1.

Part (a) Let \(L\) be a group, \(H\) be a direct factor of \(G\text{,}\) and \(\phi : H \to L\) a group homomorphism. As \(H\) is a direct factor of \(G\text{,}\) there exists some \(K\nsg G\) such that \(G=H\times K\text{.}\) Thus there exists some isomorphism \(\psi:G\to H\times K\text{.}\) Define \(\widetilde{\phi} : G \to L\) such that \(\widetilde{\phi}(g)=h\text{,}\) where \(\psi(g)=(h,k)\text{.}\) Let \(x,y\in G\text{,}\) and notice
\begin{equation*} \psi(x)=(h,k)\psi(y)=(h',k')=(hh',kk')=\psi(xy), \end{equation*}
as \(\psi\) is an isomorphism. Observe \(\widetilde{\phi}(xy)=hh'=\widetilde{\phi}(x)\widetilde{\phi}(y)\text{.}\) Thus \(\widetilde{\phi}\) is a homomorphism, and for any \(h\in H\) we have \(\widetilde{\phi}(h)=h\text{.}\)
