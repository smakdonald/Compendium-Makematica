Finally, we note that \(G, \{e\}, H_5, H_6, H_7, H_8\) are normal subgroups of \(D_8\text{,}\) since \(H_5\) is the center of \(D_8\) and each of \(H_6, H_7, H_8\) has index two. Some messy checking reveals these to be the only normal subgroups. It follows from the Fundamental Theorem that \(\Q, L, E_5, E_6, E_7, E_8\) are the only intermediate fields that are Galois over \(\Q\text{.}\) As an example, to see directly that \(E_3\) is not Galois over \(\Q\text{,}\) note that \((1+i)\sqrt[4]{2}\) is a root of \(x^4 + 4\text{,}\) which is irreducible. But \((1-i)\sqrt[4]{2}\) is also a root of this polynomial and it is not in \(E_3\text{.}\) We conclude from Proposition that \(E_3\) is not a normal extension of \(\Q\text{.}\)
