Corollary 28.10. Corollary of Maschke's Theorem.

If \(G\) is a finite group and \(K\) is a field such that \(\mathrm{char}(K) \nmid |G|\text{,}\) then every \(K\)-linear representation of \(G\) is a direct sum of irreducible representations, and every finite dimensional representation is uniquely a finite direct sum of irreducible ones.
Moreover, every irreducible representation arises as a summand of the left regular representation.
