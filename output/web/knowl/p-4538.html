Part (a) First, notice that \(A\) is upwards triangular, and thus \(\cp_A(x)=(x-3)^4\text{.}\) By the [[Mathematics/Modern Algebra/Results#Theorem – Cayley-Hamilton Theorem|Cayley-Hamilton Theorem]] the minimum polynomial divides the characteristic polynomial, and from the definition of minimum polynomial we know \(\mp_A(x)\) is the smallest polynomial such that \(\mp_A(A) = 0\text{.}\) Since \(\mp_A(x)\) must be a power of \((x-3)^n\) with \(n\leq 4\text{,}\) we plug in values of \(n\) until we get \(0\text{.}\)
