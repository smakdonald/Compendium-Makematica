To exhibit such a representation, let \(A_4\) act on \(V=\mathbb{C}^4\) by permuting the standard basis elements and thus any vector in \(V\text{.}\) The subspace \(W\subseteq V\) given by
\begin{equation*} W = \{ (a,b,c,d) \ | \ a+b+c+d=0\} \end{equation*}
is an \(A_4\)-stable subspace. This is an irreducible representation: if \(v\in W\smallsetminus 0\text{,}\) after permuting and scaling, we can write \(v=(1,x,y,-1-x-y)\text{.}\) We also have \((1,-1-x-y,x,y)\) and \((1,y,-1-x-y,x)\) in \(\langle v \rangle\text{,}\) so the sum \((3,-1,-1,-1)\in \langle v \rangle\text{.}\) Then \((-1,3,-1,-1)\) and \((-1,-1,3,-1)\) are also in \(\langle v \rangle\text{,}\) and these are three linearly independent vectors, so we must have \(\langle v \rangle=W\text{.}\)
