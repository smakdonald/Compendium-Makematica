Let \(R\) be a non-zero commutative ring and let \(V,W\text{,}\) be \(R\)-modules of finite rank \(n\) and \(m\text{,}\) respectively. (For instance, \(R\) could be a field and \(V\) and \(W\) could be arbitrary finite dimensional vector spaces.) Let \(B=\{b_1, \dots, b_n\}\) and \(C=\{c_1, \dots, c_m\}\) be ordered bases of \(V\) and \(W\text{.}\)
  1. For each \(v \in V\text{,}\) let \([v]^B\) denote the unique column vector \(\begin{bmatrix} r_1 \\ \vdots \\ r_n \end{bmatrix}\) such that \(v = \sum_i r_i b_i\text{.}\) Define \([w]^C\) for \(w \in W\) similarly.
  2. If \(f: V \to W\) is an \(R\)-module homomorphism then we define elements \(a_{ij}\in R\) for \(1\leq i\leq m\) and \(1\leq j\leq n\) by the formulas \(\begin{equation} f(b_i) = \sum_{j=1}^m a_{j,i} c_j. \end{equation}\)
