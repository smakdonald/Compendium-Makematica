Using Eisenstein's Criterion with \(p=2\text{,}\) we see that \(x^4 - 6x^2 + 10\) is irreducible in \(\Q[x]\text{.}\) However, in \(\Q(i)[x]\) observe that
\begin{equation*} x^4-6x^2+10=(x^2-(3+i))(x^2-(3-i)). \end{equation*}
As neither \((x^2-(3+i))\) nor \((x^2-(3-i))\) are units in \(\Q(i)[x]\) (as \(x\) has no inverse), we see that \(x^4-6x^2+10\) is reducible in \(\Q(i)[x]\text{.}\)
