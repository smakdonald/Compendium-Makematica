Given two normal series
\begin{equation*} \{e\} = M_0 \unlhd M_1 \unlhd M_2 \unlhd\cdots \unlhd M_j = G \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \{e\} = N_0 \unlhd N_1 \unlhd N_2 \unlhd\cdots \unlhd N_k = G \end{equation*}
of a group \(G\text{,}\) the idea is to insert a copy of each into the other. In more detail, we form a refinement of the first normal series by replacing the single step \(M_i \unlhd M_{i+1}\) with
\begin{equation*} M_i(N_0 \cap M_{i+1}) \unlhd M_i(N_1 \cap M_{i+1}) \unlhd\cdots \unlhd M_i(N_k \cap M_{i+1}) \end{equation*}
for each \(i\text{,}\) noting that \(M_i(N_0 \cap M_{i+1}) = M_i\) and \(M_i(N_k \cap M_{i+1}) = M_{i+1}\text{.}\) Create a refinement of the second normal series in an analogous way. The Butterfly Lemma implies that these two refinements are equivalent to each other.
