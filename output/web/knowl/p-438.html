For any normal subgroup \(N\) of a group \(G\text{,}\) the quotient group \(G/N\) is \(G/\sim_N\text{,}\) where \(\sim_N\) is the equivalence relation induced by the left action of \(N\) on \(G\text{.}\) In other words, \(G/N\) is the set of right cosets of \(N\) in \(G\) with multiplication given by \(Nx\cdot Ny=N(xy)\text{.}\) By part 4. of Proposition [cross-reference to target(s) "prop:normal" missing or not unique], \(G/N\) is also the set of left cosets of \(N\) in \(G\) with multiplication given by \(xN\cdot yN=(xy)N\text{.}\)
