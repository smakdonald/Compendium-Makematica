    Section D.6 June 2010

    Qualifying Exam Problems Qualifying Exam Problems

    June 2010.

    1. Problem 1.

    Recall that the centralizer of a subgroup \(H\) in a group \(G\) is
    \begin{equation*} C_{G}(H)=\{g \in G \mid g h=h g \text { for all } h \in H\} \text {. } \end{equation*}
    1. Prove that if \(H\) is normal in \(G\text{,}\) then \(C_{G}(H)\) is normal in \(G\text{.}\)
    2. Prove that if \(H\) is normal in \(G\text{,}\) then \(G / C_{G}(H)\) is isomorphic to a subgroup of \(\operatorname{Aut}(H)\) (the group of automorphisms of \(H\) ).
    Solution.

    2. Problem 2.

    1. Suppose \(H\) is a subgroup of a group \(G\) and \([G: H]=7\text{.}\) Prove \(G\) contains a normal subgroup \(N\) such that \(N \subset H\) and \([G: N] \leq 7\) !.
    2. Prove \(7!\) is the best possible bound for the previous part - i.e., prove there is a group \(G\) and a subgroup \(H\) with \([G: H]=7\) such that for every normal subgroup \(N\) of \(G\) with \(N \subset H\text{,}\) we have \([G: N] \geq 7\) !.
    Solution.

    3. Problem 3.

    Suppose \(G\) is a simple group of order \(168=2^{3} \cdot 3 \cdot 7\text{.}\) (Yes, there is such a group.)
    1. How many elements of order 7 does \(G\) have?
    2. Show that \(G\) has at least 14 elements of order 3.
    Solution.

    4. Problem 4.

    Let \(A=\left[\begin{array}{llll}3 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 1 & 3\end{array}\right]\text{,}\) with entries in \(\mathbb{C}\text{.}\)
    1. Find the Jordan canonical form of \(A\text{.}\)
    2. Let \(B=\left[\begin{array}{cccc}0 & -9 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 6 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -9 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 6\end{array}\right]\text{,}\) with entries in \(\mathbb{C}\text{.}\) Is \(A\) similar to \(B\) ?
    Solution.

    5. Problem 5.

    Let \(R\) be a ring with identity and let \(M\) be a left \(R\)-module. Recall that the annihilator of \(M\) in \(R\) is
    \begin{equation*} \operatorname{ann}_{R}(M)=\{r \in R \mid r m=0 \text { for all } m \in M\} . \end{equation*}
    1. Prove that \(\operatorname{ann}_{R}(M)\) is a 2 -sided ideal of \(R\text{.}\)
    2. Suppose \(M\) is an abelian group (i.e., a \(\mathbb{Z}\)-module) such that \(|M|=400\) and \(\operatorname{ann}_{\mathbb{Z}}(M)\) is the ideal generated by 20. How many possibilities, up to isomorphism, are there for \(M\) ?
    Solution.

    6. Problem 6.

    Let \(F\) be a field and \(V\) a vector space (not necessarily finite-dimensional) over \(F\text{.}\) Prove that every linearly independent subset of \(V\) is contained in a basis for \(V\text{.}\)
    Solution.
    See: Item 2 of Corollary 13.41.

    7. Problem 7.

    Let \(R\) be an integral domain with field of fractions \(Q\text{.}\) Let \(P\) be a prime ideal of \(R\) and let
    \begin{equation*} S=\left\{\left.\frac{r}{d} \in Q \right\rvert\, d \notin P\right\} \end{equation*}
    1. Show that \(S\) is a subring of \(Q\text{.}\)
    2. Show that
      \begin{equation*} I=\left\{\left.\frac{p}{d} \right\rvert\, p \in P, d \notin P\right\} \end{equation*}
      is a prime ideal of \(S\text{.}\)
    Solution.

    8. Problem 8.

    Prove \(\mathbb{Z}[2 i]=\{a+2 b i \mid a\) and \(b\) are integers \(\}\) is not a PID. Hint: One method is to use (with proof) the fact that \(2+2 i\) is irreducible in this ring.
    Solution.

    9. Problem 9.

    Consider \(f(x)=x^{6}+3 \in \mathbb{Q}[x]\text{.}\)
    1. Let \(\alpha\) be a root of \(f(x)\) and prove \(\mathbb{Q}(\alpha)\) is Galois over \(\mathbb{Q}\text{.}\) (Hint: First show \(\frac{\alpha^{3}+1}{2}\) is a primitive 6 -th root of unity.)
    2. Find the Galois group of \(\mathbb{Q}(\alpha) / \mathbb{Q}\text{.}\)
    Solution.

    June 2011.

    1. Problem 1.

    Prove that no group of order 150 is simple.
    Solution.

    2. Problem 2.

    Let \(G\) be a finite group.
    1. If \(N\) is a normal subgroup of \(G\) and \(|N|=2\text{,}\) prove that \(N\) is contained in the center \(\mathrm{Z}(G)\) of \(G\text{.}\)
    2. Suppose that \(|\mathrm{Z}(G)|\) is odd and that \(G\) contains a non-trivial simple subgroup \(H\) with \([G: H]=2\text{.}\) Prove that \(H\) is the only non-trivial proper normal subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)
    Solution.

    3. Problem 3.

    Let \(H\) be a normal subgroup of a finite group \(G, p\) a prime dividing the order of \(H\text{,}\) and \(P\) a Sylow \(p\)-subgroup of \(H\text{.}\) Prove that \(G=H \mathrm{~N}_{G}(P)\text{.}\) [Hint: For \(g \in G\text{,}\) consider the subgroup \(g P g^{-1}\text{.}\)]
    Solution.

    4. Problem 4.

    Fix a prime number \(p\text{,}\) and let \(A\) denote the abelian group of all complex roots of unity whose orders are powers of \(p\text{;}\) that is,
    \begin{equation*} A=\left\{z \in \mathbb{C} \mid z^{p^{n}}=1 \text { for some integer } n \geq 1\right\} \text {. } \end{equation*}
    Prove the following statements.
    1. Every non-trivial subgroup of \(A\) contains the group of \(p^{\text {th }}\) roots of unity.
    2. Every proper subgroup of \(A\) is cyclic.
    3. If \(B\) and \(C\) are subgroups of \(A\text{,}\) then either \(B \subseteq C\) or \(C \subseteq B\text{.}\)
    4. For each \(n \geq 0\) there exists a unique subgroup of \(A\) with \(p^{n}\) elements.
    Solution.

    5. Problem 5.

    Let \(F\) be a field, and let \(f(x) \in F[x]\text{.}\) Recall that \(f(x)\) is separable provided, for every extension field \(K / F, f(x)\) has no multiple roots in \(K\text{.}\) (A multiple root is an element \(\alpha \in K\) such that \((x-\alpha)^{2} \mid f(x)\) in \(K[x]\text{.}\))
    1. Prove that \(f(x) \in F[x]\) is separable if and only if \(f(x)\) and its derivative \(f^{\prime}(x)\) are relatively prime in \(F[x]\text{.}\)
