Definition 15.33. Jordan Canonical Form.

Let \(F\) be a field, \(V\) be a finite dimensional vector space, and let \(t: V \rightarrow V\) be a linear transformation satisfying the property that the characteristic polynomial \(c_{t}\) of \(t\) factors completely into linear factors and has elementary divisors \(\left(x-r_{1}\right)^{e_{1}}, \ldots,\left(x-r_{s}\right)^{e_{s}}\text{.}\) The matrix \(J_{e_{1}}\left(r_{1}\right) \oplus \cdots \oplus J_{e_{s}}\left(r_{s}\right)\) is a Jordan canonical form (JCF) of \(t\text{.}\)
