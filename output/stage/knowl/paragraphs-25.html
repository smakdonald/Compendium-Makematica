Section I: Group Theory

Problem F.55. Problem 1.

Let \(G\) be a finite group.
  1. Suppose every subgroup of \(G\) is normal. Prove that given any positive divisor \(d\) of \(|G|\) there exists a subgroup of \(G\) of order \(d\text{.}\)
  2. Give an example, with justification, of a finite group \(G\) and a positive divisor \(d\) of \(|G|\) such that \(G\) has no subgroup of order \(d\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.56. Problem 2.

Let \(G\) be a group of order \(p^n\) for some prime \(p\) acting on a finite set \(X\text{.}\)
  1. Suppose \(p\) does not divide \(|X|\text{.}\) Prove that there exists some element of \(X\) fixed by all elements of \(G\text{.}\)
  2. Suppose \(G\) acts faithfully on \(X\text{.}\) Prove that \(|X| \geq np\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.57. Problem 3.

Prove that any group of order \(3^2\cdot 11\cdot 17\) is abelian.
Solution.

Problem F.58. Problem 4.

Let \(R\) be a commutative domain.
  1. State the definition for \(R\) to be a Euclidean domain.
  2. Prove that in a Euclidean domain every ideal is principal.
Solution.

Problem F.59. Problem 5.

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring in which every element \(x\) satisfies \(x^n = x\) for some \(n > 1.\) Show that every prime ideal in R is maximal.
Solution.

Problem F.60. Problem 6.

Let \(n\) be a positive integer and let \(J = J_{2n}(0)\) be the Jordan block matrix of size \(2n \times 2n\) with eigenvalue 0 in \(M_{2n\times2n}(\C)\text{.}\)
  1. Find the minimal polynomials for \(J\) and for \(J^2\text{,}\) with justification.
  2. Find the Jordan canonical form of \(J^2\text{,}\) with justification. Hint: consider the kernel of \(J^2\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.61. Problem 7.

Let \(L/F\) be a field extension and let \(K := \{a\in L | a \text{ is algebraic over }F \}\text{.}\) Show that if \(L\) is algebraically closed, then K is algebraically closed.
Solution.

Problem F.62. Problem 8.

Let \(n\) be a positive integer and let \(p\) be a prime integer. Consider the polynomial
\begin{equation*} q(x) =x^{p^n}-x \in(\Z/p)[x] \end{equation*}
and define \(K\) to be the splitting field of \(q\) over \(\Z/p\text{.}\)
  1. Show that the set \(E\) of roots of \(q(x)\) in \(K\) is a subfield of \(K\text{.}\)
  2. Show that \(K\) has exactly \(p^n\) elements.
Solution.

Problem F.63. Problem 9.

Consider \(f (x) = x^5-4x-2 \in \Q[x]\text{.}\) This polynomial has exactly three real roots, a fact that you may use without proof.
  1. Show that \(f\) is irreducible in \(\Q[x]\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(L\) be a splitting field of \(f\) over \(\Q\text{.}\) Show that \(L/\Q\) is a Galois extension with Galois group \(\Gal(L/\Q)\) isomorphic to the symmetric group \(S_5\text{.}\)
Solution.
