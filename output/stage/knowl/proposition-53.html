Proposition 14.23.

Let \(F\) be a field and let \(V\) and \(W\) be finite dimensional \(F\)-vector spaces.
  1. Let \(\a: V \to W\) be an \(F\)-linear transformation. Prove \(\dim(\coker(\a)) - \dim(\ker(\a)) = \dim(W) - \dim(V)\text{,}\) where by definition \(\coker(\a) := W/\im(\a)\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(\phi: V \to V\) be an \(F\)-linear transformation from \(V\) to itself. Prove that if \(\phi \circ \phi = 0\text{,}\) then \(\rank(\phi) \leq \frac12 \dim_F(V)\text{.}\) (Note: Recall that, by definition, \(\rank(\phi) := \dim_F(\im(\phi))\text{.}\))
  3. For extra credit: Let \(\phi: V \to V\) be an \(F\)-linear transformation from \(V\) to itself. Prove that if \(\overbrace{\phi \circ \cdots \circ \phi}^{\text{n times}} = 0\) for some \(n \geq 1\) then \(\rank(\phi) \leq \frac{n-1}{n} \dim_F(V)\text{.}\)
