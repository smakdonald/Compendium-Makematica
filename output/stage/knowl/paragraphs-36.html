Section I: Group Theory

Problem F.156. Problem 1.

Let \(G\) be a group. A subgroup \(H\) of \(G\) is called maximal if \(H\neq G\) (that is, \(H\) is a proper subgroup of \(G\)) and whenever \(K\) is another subgroup of \(G\) containing \(H\text{,}\) either \(K = H\) or \(K = G\text{.}\) Show that every nontrivial finitely generated group \(G\) possesses maximal subgroups.
Solution.

Problem F.157. Problem 2.

Let \(G\) be a group and \(H\) a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) Recall that the centralizer of \(H\) in \(G\) is
\begin{equation*} C_H(G)=\{g\in G|gh=hg\text{ for all }h\in H\} \end{equation*}
Prove that if \(H\) is normal in \(G\text{,}\) then so is \(C_G(H)\) and that \(G/C_G(H)\) is isomorphic to a subgroup of the automorphism group of \(H\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.158. Problem 3.

Let \(G\) be a group of order \(p^2q\) where \(p\) and \(q\) are distinct primes. (a) Prove that \(G\) contains a normal [[Mathematics/Definitions/Sylow p-Subgroup|Sylow subgroup]]. (b) Suppose \(p < q\) and the Sylow \(p\)-subgroup is cyclic and normal. Prove that \(G\) is abelian.
Solution.

Problem F.159. Problem 3?

Let \(G\) be a finite group of order \(p^2q\) with \(p < q\) prime numbers. Show that \(G\) is not a simple group.
Solution.

Problem F.160. Problem 4.

Consider the [[Mathematics/Definitions/Subring|subring]] \(\Z[\sqrt{10}] = \{a + b\sqrt{10} | a, b \in \Z\}\) of \(\R\text{.}\) Show that \(2\) is irreducible but not prime in \(\Z[\sqrt{10}].\) Hint: Consider the function \(N : \Z[\sqrt{10}]\to \Z, N (a + b\sqrt{10}) = a^2-10b^2\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.161. Problem 5.

Recall that a \(\Z\)-module \(M\) is called torsion-free if its torsion submodule is \(\Tor(M ) =\{0\},\) where \(\Tor(M ) = \{m \in M | rm = 0 \text{ for some }r \in \Z\setminus\{0\}\}.\) Consider the \(\Z\)-module
\begin{equation*} M= \frac{\Z \oplus \Z}{\langle(7, 11)\rangle }\text{ where }\langle(7, 11)\rangle = \{(7k, 11k) | k \in \Z\}. \end{equation*}
Show that \(M\) is torsion free.
Solution.

Problem F.162. Problem 6.

Let \(n\) be a positive integer. Consider the real vector space \(V = \{p \in R[x] | \deg(p) \leq n\}\) and the [[Mathematics/Definitions/Linear Transformation|linear transformation]] \(T : V \to V, T (p) = p'\text{,}\) where \(p'(x)\) is the [[Mathematics/Definitions/Derivative|derivative]] of p(x).
  1. Find the characteristic polynomial and the minimal polynomial for \(T\text{.}\)
  2. Find the [[Mathematics/Definitions/Invariant Factor|invariant factors]] and the [[Mathematics/Definitions/Elementary Divisor|elementary divisors]] for \(T\text{.}\)
  3. Find the [[Theorem – Rational Canonical Form|rational canonical form]] and the [[Theorem – Jordan Canonical Form|Jordan canonical form]] for \(T\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.163. Problem 7.

Let \(E, K,\) and \(L\) be subfields of a field \(F\) with \(E \subseteq K\) and \(E \subseteq L\text{.}\) Let \(k = [K : E]\) and \(\ell = [L : E]\text{.}\) Recall that \(KL\) denotes the smallest (with respect to containment) subfield of \(F\) which satisfies \(K\subseteq KL\) and \(L\subseteq KL\text{.}\)
  1. Show that \([KL : E] \leq k\ell\text{.}\)
  2. Show that if \(\gcd(k,\ell) = 1\) then \([KL : E] = k\ell.\)
  3. Give an example satisfying \([KL : E] < k\ell\)
Solution.

Problem F.164. Problem 8.

Let \(F\subseteq L\) be fields and let \(x, y \in L\) be algebraic elements over \(F\text{.}\) Prove that \(x + y\) and \(xy\) are also algebraic elements of \(L\) over \(F\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.165. Problem 9.

Consider \(f (x) = x^5 -20x - 2 \in \Q[x].\) This polynomial has exactly three real roots, a fact that you may use without proof.
  1. Show that \(f\) is irreducible in \(\Q[x]\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(L\) be a splitting field of \(f\) $over \(\Q\text{.}\) Show that \(L/\Q\) is a Galois extension and find the isomorphism class of the Galois group \(\Gal(L/\Q)\text{.}\)
