4. Problem 4.

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring with identity, and assume \(1 \neq 0\text{.}\) Let \(I\) and \(J\) be ideals such that \(I+J=R\text{.}\)
  1. Prove \(I J=I \cap J\text{.}\)
  2. Prove the following special case of the Chinese Remainder Theorem: There is an isomorphism of rings of the form \(R /(I \cap J) \cong R / I \times R / J\text{.}\)
Solution.

5. Problem 5.

Prove the Rank-Nullity Theorem: If \(F\) is a field, \(V\) and \(W\) are a finite dimensional \(F\)-vector spaces, and \(g: V \rightarrow W\) is an \(F\)-linear transformation, then \(\operatorname{rank}(g)+\operatorname{nullity}(g)=\operatorname{dim}(V)\text{.}\) (Recall the rank of \(g\) is the dimension of its image and the nullity of \(g\) is the dimension of its kernel.)
Solution.

6. Problem 6.

Let \(F\) be a field and \(n\) a positive integer. We say an \(n \times n\) matrix \(A\) with entries in \(F\) is unipotent if \(A-I_{n}\) is nilpotent (i.e., \(\left(A-I_{n}\right)^{k}=0\) for some \(k \geq 1\) ). For the field \(F=\mathbb{Q}\text{,}\) find (with complete justification) the number of similarity classes of \(4 \times 4\) unipotent matrices and give an explicit representative for each class.
Solution.

7. Problem 7.

  2. Let \(F=\mathbb{Q}(\zeta)\) where \(\zeta=e^{2 \pi i / 5}\text{,}\) a primitive 5 -th root of unity. Prove \(x^{5}-11\) is irreducible in \(F[x]\text{.}\)
Solution.
