Theorem 1.19. Laws of Exponents.

Let \(m,n\in\Z\) and \(x\) be an element of a group \(G\text{.}\) Then
  1. \(x^mx^n=x^{m+n}=x^nx^m\) and
  2. \((x^m)^n=x^{mn}=(x^n)^m\text{.}\)

Proof.

  1. Let \(m > 0\) and \(n > 0\text{;}\) then by induction on \(n\) and the definition \(x^mx^n =x^{m + n}\text{.}\) Multiplying on both sides by \(x^{-m}\) we deduce that \(x^n =x^{-m}x^{m+n}\) and \(x^m = x^{m + n} x^{-n}\text{.}\) Finally inversion of the equation \(x^mx^n =x^{m + n}\) yields \(x^{-n}x^{-m} = x^{-m + (-n)}\text{.}\) Hence the law is established in all cases.
  2. If \(n > 0\text{,}\) it follows from (1) that \((x^m)^n =x^{mn}\text{.}\) Now assume that \(n < 0\text{;}\) then \((x^m)^n =((x^m)^{-n})\inv =(x^{-mn})\inv =x^{mn}\) since \(x^{-mn}x^{mn}=e\text{.}\)
