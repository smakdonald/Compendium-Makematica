Exercise 1.30. Order of \(S_n\).

Prove \(|S_n|=n!\text{.}\)
Solution.
To count the number of permutations in \(S_n\text{,}\) we can consider the process of constructing a permutation step by step.
  • For the first element, we have \(n\) choices. We can choose any of the \(n\) elements as the image of the first element.
  • For the second element, we have \((n-1)\) choices. After choosing the image of the first element, we have \((n-1)\) remaining elements to choose from for the image of the second element.
  • Continuing this process, for the third element, we have \((n-2)\) choices, for the fourth element we have \((n-3)\) choices, and so on.
  • Finally, for the last element, we have only one choice remaining.
The total number of permutations is obtained by multiplying the number of choices at each step. Hence, the total number of permutations in \(S_n\) is given by \(n \cdot (n-1) \cdot (n-2) \cdot \dots \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 1\text{,}\) which is equal to \(n!\text{.}\) Therefore, the order of the symmetric group \(S_n\) is \(n!\text{.}\)
