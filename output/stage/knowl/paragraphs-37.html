Suppose that for all \(g\in G\) we have \(g^2\in H\text{.}\) Prove the following: (a) \(H\) is a subgroup of \(G\) (b) \(H\) is normal (c) \(G/H\) (See: [[Mathematics/Definitions/Coset]]) is abelian.

Problem F.170. Problem 5.

Suppose that \(T : V\to V\) is a [[Mathematics/Definitions/Linear Transformation|linear map]], where \(V\) is a finite dimensional \(\C\)-vector space. Fix a polynomial \(p\in \C[x]\text{.}\)
  1. Prove that if \(\lambda\) is an [[Mathematics/Definitions/Eigenvalue|eigenvalue]] of \(T\) then \(p(\lambda)\) is an eigenvalue of \(p(T)\text{.}\)
  2. Prove conversely that if \(\mu\) is an eigenvalue of \(p(T)\) then there exists an eigenvalue \(\l\) of \(T\) such that \(\mu = p(\l)\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.172. Problem 7.

Assume that \(F \sse K\) is a finite extension of fields of degree \(n = [K : F ]\text{.}\)
  1. Prove that if \(f \in F [x]\) is irreducible of degree \(d\) and \(\gcd(d, n) = 1\) then \(f\) remains irreducible when regarded as an element of the ring \(K[x]\text{.}\)
  2. Show, by means of an explicit example with justification, that the statement in part (a) would become false if the assumption that \(\gcd(n, d) = 1\) were omitted.
Solution.

Problem F.173. Problem 8.

Suppose that \(F \sse L\) is a finite Galois extension with Galois group \(G\text{,}\) and that \(\a \in L\text{.}\) Prove that \(L = F (\a)\) if and only if the images of \(\a\) under elements of \(G\) are distinct.
Solution.
