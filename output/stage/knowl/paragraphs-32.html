Section I: Group Theory

Problem F.120. Problem 1.

Let \(p\) be a prime. (a) Prove that every group of order \(p^2\) is abelian. (Provide as many details as you can, but you may use main theorems – such as Lagrange's Theorem or the Class Equation – without proof.) (b) Prove that there exists a non-abelian group of order \(p^3\) for any prime \(p\text{.}\) (You may use facts about automorphism groups here without proof, although they should be clearly stated.)
Solution.

Problem F.121. Problem 2.

Let \(G\) be a group of order \(2m\) for some odd integer \(m > 1\text{.}\) Let \(\Perm(G)\) be the group of permutations on the elements of \(G\) (i.e., the group of bijective functions from G to G). You may use without proof that \(\Perm(G)\cong S_{2m}\text{.}\)
  1. Let \(f : G → \Perm(G)\) be given by \(f (g) = λg\) for all \(g ∈ G\text{,}\) where \(λg : G → G\) is defined by \(λg(a) = ga\) for all \(a ∈ G\text{.}\) Prove that \(f\) is an injective group homomorphism.
  2. Prove that if \(g ∈ G\) is an element of order \(2\) then \(λg\) is a product of \(m\) transpositions.
  3. Prove \(G\) contains a subgroup of index \(2\) and is consequently not simple. (Tip: Consider the inverse image under \(f\) of the subgroup of \(\Perm(G)\) consisting of the even permutations.)
Solution.

Problem F.122. Problem 3.

Let \(G\) be a finite group and \(p\) a prime dividing the order of \(G\text{.}\) Let P be a Sylow \(p\)-subgroup. (a) Suppose \(Q\) is a Sylow \(p\)-subgroup distinct from \(P\text{.}\) Prove that \(PQ\) is not a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) (Recall that \(PQ = {pq | p ∈ P, q ∈ Q}\text{.}\)) (b) Prove that \(N_G(P ) = N_G(N_G(P ))\) where \(N_G(H)\) denotes the normalizer in \(G\) of a subgroup \(H\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.123. Problem 4.

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring with identity, and assume \(1\neq0\text{.}\) Let \(I\) and \(J\) be ideals such that \(I + J = R\text{.}\)
  1. Prove \(IJ = I\cap J\text{.}\)
  2. Prove the following special case of the Sunzi's Remainder Theorem: There is an isomorphism of rings of the form \(R/(I \cap J)\cong R/I \times R/J\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.124. Problem 5.

Let \(R\) be a non-zero, unital ring, and let \(R^m\) and \(R^n\) be the standard free left \(R\)-modules of finite rank \(m\) and \(n\text{.}\) Assume there is an isomorphism of \(R\)-modules \(R^m \cong R^n.\)
  1. Prove that if \(R\) is commutative then \(m = n\text{.}\) You may assume without justification that this holds in the special case when \(R\) is a field.
  2. Show, by example, than \(m\) need not equal \(n\) if \(R\) is not assumed to be commutative.
Solution.

Problem F.125. Problem 6.

Find all the ideals of \(\Z[x]\) that contain \((6, x^2 + x + 1)\text{,}\) the ideal of \(\Z[x]\) generated by \(6\) and \(x^2 + x + 1\text{.}\) For each such ideal, give an explicit list of generators, and determine whether the ideal is prime, maximal, or neither.
Solution.

Problem F.126. Problem 7.

Suppose \(F\) is any field. Recall that a square matrix \(A\) with entries in \(F\) is nilpotent if \(A^j = 0\) for some positive integer j.
  1. Prove that if \(A\in Mat_{n\times n}(F)\) and \(A\) is nilpotent, then \(A^n = 0\text{.}\)
  2. Find, with justification, the number of similarity classes of \(5 \times 5\) nilpotent matrices with entries in \(F\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.127. Problem 8.

Let \(L\) be the splitting field over \(\Q\) of the polynomial \(f(x) = x^5 + 2 \in \Q[x].\) (a) Find, with justification, \([L : \Q]\text{.}\) (b) Note that \(w=-\sqrt[5]{2}\) (where \(\sqrt[5]{2}\) is the unique positive, real fifth root of \(2\)) is one of the roots of \(f (x)\text{.}\) Prove \(\Aut(\Q(w)/\Q)\) is the trivial group.
Solution.

Problem F.128. Problem 9.

Assume \(F\) is field and let \(f(x)\in F [x]\text{.}\) Recall that \(f (x)\) is separable if \(f (x)\) has no repeated roots in an [[Mathematics/Definitions/Algebraic Closure|algebraic closure]] of \(F\text{.}\)
  1. Assume \(\char(F) = 0\text{.}\) Prove that \(f(x)\) is separable if and only if the irreducible factorization of \(f(x)\) in \(F[x]\) has no repeated factors.
  2. Fix a prime integer \(p\text{,}\) let \(\F_p\) be the field with \(p\) elements, and let \(F\) be the [[Mathematics/Definitions/Field of Fractions|field of fractions]] of the polynomial ring \(\F_{p}[y]\text{.}\) Prove \(x^p-y\) is irreducible in \(F[x]\) but not separable.
Solution.
