Consider the matrix
\begin{equation*} A=\left[\begin{array}{ccc} 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 3 & 9 & 5 \\ 1 & -2 & 7 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
What \(\mathbb{Z}\)-module \(M\) is presented by \(A\text{?}\) Formally, \(M\) is the quotient module \(M=\mathbb{Z}^{4} / \operatorname{im}\left(t_{A}\right)\text{,}\) where \(t_{A}: \mathbb{Z}^{3} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}^{4}\) is defined by \(t_{A}(v)=A v\text{.}\) Since \(\mathbb{Z}^{4}\) is generated by its standard basis elements \(\left\{e_{1}, e_{2}, e_{3}, e_{4}\right\}\text{,}\) we deduce as in LemmaÂ 12.53 that \(M=\mathbb{Z}^{4} / \operatorname{im}\left(t_{A}\right)\) is generated by the cosets of the \(e_{i}\text{.}\) To keep the notation short, we set \(m_{i}=e_{i}+\operatorname{im}\left(t_{A}\right)\text{.}\)
