Summary 3.2.

  • A subgroup is a Normal Subgroup if \(gNg^{-1} = N\) for all \(g \in G\text{,}\) though there are several Equivalent Normal Characterizations.
  • Abelian subgroups are always normal; in particular, the center of a group is always normal.
  • If a \(H\leq G\) is the only subgroup of order \(n\text{,}\) then \(H\nsg G\text{.}\)  1 
  • The Converse to Lagrange's Theorem is False.
