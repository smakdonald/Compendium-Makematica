Part (b) \((\Rightarrow)\) Suppose \(T\) is maximal linearly independent, and suppose by way of contradiction that \(M/N\) is not torsion. Thus there exists some \(xN\in M/N\) such that for all \(r\in R\text{,}\) we see that \(rx\neq0\text{.}\) However, as \(x\neq 0\) and \(x\not\in N\text{,}\) this means that \(x\not\in T\text{.}\) Consider \(T\cup\{x\}\text{.}\) This set is linearly independent, contradicting the assumption that \(T\) was maximal.
