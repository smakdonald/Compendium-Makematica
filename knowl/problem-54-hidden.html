Let \(G\) be a group with subgroups \(H, K\text{.}\) Recall the notation
\begin{equation*} HK=\{hk \mid h\in H, k\in K\}. \end{equation*}
(a) Prove that \(HK\leq G\) if and only if \(HK=KH\text{.}\) (b) Prove that if either one of \(H\) or \(K\) is a normal subgroup of \(G\text{,}\) then \(HK\leq G\) and \(HK=KH=\langle H\cup K\rangle\text{.}\)
