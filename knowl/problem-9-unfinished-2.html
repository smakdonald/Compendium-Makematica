Consider the following matrix over \(\Q\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix}-1&0&0&0\\0&-1&0&0\\-2&-2&0&1\\-2&0&-1&-2\end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
(a) Show that the characteristic and minimal polynomials of \(A\) are, respectively, \((x + 1)^4\) and \((x + 1)^3\text{.}\) (b) Find the rational canonical form \(R\) of \(A\) and the Jordan canonical form \(J\) of \(A\text{.}\) (c) Find an invertible matrix \(P\) such that \(P\inv AP = J\text{.}\)
Proof..
Part (a).
Let \(A\) denote the above matrix.
Part (b).
As \(\cp_A(x)=(x + 1)^4\) and \(\mp_A(x)=(x + 1)^3\text{,}\) we know that our characteristic polynomial factors into linear factors, so \(A\) does indeed have a JCF. This also means that \(A\) only has one elementary divisor, \((x + 1)^4\text{,}\) which corresponds to the Jordan Block \(J_4(-1)\text{,}\) a matrix with \(-1\)s along the diagonal, \(1\)s along the subdiagonal, and \(0\) everywhere else. This is the JCF of \(A\text{.}\)
