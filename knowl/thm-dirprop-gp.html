Theorem 1.12. Direct Products of Groups.

  1. Let \((G,+)\) and \((H,\times)\) be groups. Then \(G\times H\) is a group, where \((G\times H,*)\) denotes the Cartesian Product of \(G\) and \(H\text{,}\) and the operation \(*\) is defined
    \begin{equation*} (g_1,h_1)*(g_2,h_2)=(g_1+g_2,h_1\times h_2). \end{equation*}
  2. Let \(G_1,\dots,G_n\) be groups. Then \(G_1\times\cdots\times G_n\) is a group, with its operation defined similary to that in part (1).
