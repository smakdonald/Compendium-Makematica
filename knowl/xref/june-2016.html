1. Problem 1.

  1. Let \(G\) be a (not necessarily finite) group and \(K \leq G\) a subgroup of index \(n<\infty\text{.}\) Define
\begin{equation*} N=\bigcap_{g \in G} g K g^{-1} \end{equation*}
(i.e., \(N\) is the interesection of all the conjugates of \(K\) ).
(a) (10 points) Prove \(N\) is a the largest normal subgroup of \(G\) that is contained in \(K\text{.}\)
(b) (10 points) Prove \([G: N]\) divides \(n\) !.
Solution.

4. Problem 4.

  1. Let \(L\) be the splitting field over \(\mathbb{Q}\) of the polynomial \(x^{3}-7\text{.}\)
(a) (10 points) Find all intermediate fields \(E\) with \(\mathbb{Q} \subseteq E \subseteq L\) (including possibly \(L\) and \(\mathbb{Q}\) ) such that \(E\) is Galois over \(\mathbb{Q}\text{.}\)
(b) (10 points) For each field \(E\) you found in (a), find with justification a primitive generator (i.e., find \(\alpha \in E\) so that \(E=\mathbb{Q}(\alpha))\text{.}\)
Solution.

7. Problem 7.

  1. Let \(F\) be a field and recall that a square matrix \(A\) with entries in \(F\) is called nilpotent if \(A^{m}=0\) for some positive integer \(m\text{.}\)
(a) ( 7 points) Prove that if \(A\) is an \(n \times n\) nilpotent matrix, then \(A^{n}=0\text{.}\)
(b) ( 7 points) Assume \(n \leq 3\) and prove that two \(n \times n\) nilpotent matrices are similar if and only if they have the same rank. (Recall the rank of a matrix is the dimension of the vector space spanned by its columns.)
(c) (6 points) Give an example, with justification, of two \(4 \times 4\) nilpotent matrices that have the same rank but are not similar.
Solution.
