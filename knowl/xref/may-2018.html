1. Problem 1.

Let \(H\) and \(K\) be groups. Recall that for any group \(G\text{,}\) an automorphism of \(G\) is an isomorphism from \(G\) to \(G\text{,}\) and \(\operatorname{Aut}(G)\) denotes the group of automorphisms of \(G\text{.}\)
(a) [14 points] Show that the direct product group \(\operatorname{Aut}(H) \times \operatorname{Aut}(K)\) is isomorphic to a subgroup of \(\operatorname{Aut}(H \times K)\text{.}\)
(b) [6 points] Give an example, with justification, of groups \(H\) and \(K\) for which \(\operatorname{Aut}(H) \times \operatorname{Aut}(K)\) is not isomorphic to \(\operatorname{Aut}(H \times K)\text{.}\)
Solution.

2. Problem 2.

Let \(K\) be a normal subgroup of a group \(H\text{.}\) Recall that a group \(G\) is solvable if there is a sequence of subgroups
\begin{equation*} 1=N_{0} \unlhd N_{1} \unlhd \cdots \unlhd N_{j}=G \end{equation*}
for some \(j \geq 0\) such that for all \(i \in\{0, \ldots, j-1\}\) the quotient group \(N_{i+1} / N_{i}\) is abelian. Show that if both \(K\) and \(H / K\) are solvable groups, then \(H\) is solvable.
Solution.
