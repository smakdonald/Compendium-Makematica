1. Problem 1.

Let \(G\) be a finite group and let \(H\) be a subgroup of \(G\) of index \(p\text{,}\) where \(p\) is the smallest prime dividing the order of \(G\text{.}\) Prove that \(H\) is a normal subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)
Hint: Use an action of \(G\) on the set of cosets of \(H\text{.}\)

2. Problem 2.

  1. Give two groups of order 21 which are not isomorphic, with justification.
  2. Prove that there are at most three groups of order 21 up to isomorphism.
Solution.

3. Problem 3.

Let \(G\) be a finite abelian group and call the exponent of \(G\) the smallest positive integer \(n\) which satisfies \(g^{n}=e_{G}\) for all \(g \in G\text{,}\) where \(e_{G}\) is the identity element of \(G\text{.}\)
  1. Give, with justification, a formula for the exponent of \(G\) in terms of the invariant factors of \(G\text{.}\) (As a reminder for those who confuse "invariant factors" and "elementary divisors": the invariant factors form a list in which each one divides the next.)
  2. For \(p\) prime, determine the exponent of the abelian group \(((\mathbb{Z} / p) \backslash\{0\}, \cdot)\) and use it to show that this group is cyclic.
Solution.

Solution.

5. Problem 5.

Solution.

6. Problem 6.

are similar.
Solution.

7. Problem 7.

Let \(K \subseteq L\) be a finite extension of fields and assume \(f(x)\) is a polynomial with coefficients in \(K\) that is irreducible in the ring \(K[x]\text{.}\) Prove \(f(x)\) remains irreducible when regarded as an element of the ring \(L[x]\) provided \([L: K]\) is relatively prime to the degree of \(f(x)\text{.}\)
Solution.

8. Problem 8.

Let \(L\) be the splitting field of \(x^{3}-2\) over \(\mathbb{Q}\text{.}\)
  1. Prove there is a unique intermediate field \(\mathbb{Q} \subseteq K \subseteq L\) such that \([K: \mathbb{Q}]=\) 2.
  2. Find, with justification, a primitive generator for the field \(K\) you found in part (a); that is, find an explicit element \(\alpha \in K\) such that \(K=\mathbb{Q}(\alpha)\text{.}\)
