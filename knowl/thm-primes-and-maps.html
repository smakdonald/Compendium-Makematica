Theorem 9.33. Prime Ideals and Ring Maps.

  1. If \(R\) is a domain, \(S\) is a ring and \(\varphi:R\to S\) is a ring homomorphism, then \(\ker(\varphi)\) is a prime ideal.
  2. If \(\varphi:A\to B\) is a ring homomorphism and \(P\) is a prime ideal in \(B\text{,}\) then \(\varphi\inv(P)\) is prime in \(A\text{.}\)
