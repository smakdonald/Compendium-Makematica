Example 3.23. The Infinite Dihedral Group.

The infinite dihedral group \(D_\infty\) is the set \(D_\infty = \{r^i,r^is \mid i \in \mathbb{Z}\}\) with multiplication defined by \((r^i)(r^j) = r^{i+j}, (r^i)(r^js) = r^{i+j}s, (r^is)(r^j) = r^{i-j}s\text{,}\) and \((r^is)(r^js) = r^{i-j}\text{.}\) In other words, \(D_\infty\) is the group having presentation
\begin{equation*} D_\infty=\langle r,s \mid s^2=e, srs=r^{-1}\rangle. \end{equation*}
Then \(\langle r^n \rangle \mathrel{\unlhd}D_\infty\) and \(D_\infty/ \langle r^n \rangle \cong D_{2n}\) via \(r\langle r^n \rangle\mapsto r\) and \(s\langle r^n \rangle\mapsto s\text{.}\)
