Problem F.92. Problem 2.

Let \(G\) be a finite group. (a) If \(N\) is a normal subgroup of \(G\) and \(|N | = 2\text{,}\) prove that \(N\) is contained in the center \(Z(G)\) of \(G\text{.}\) (b) Suppose that \(| Z(G)|\) is odd and that \(G\) contains a non-trivial simple subgroup \(H\) with \([G : H] = 2\text{.}\) Prove that \(H\) is the only non-trivial proper normal subgroup of \(G\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.93. Problem 3.

Let \(H\) be a normal subgroup of a finite group \(G\text{,}\) \(p\) a prime dividing the order of \(H\text{,}\) and \(P\) a Sylow \(p\)-subgroup of \(H\text{.}\) Prove that \(G = H N_G(P )\) Hint: For \(g \in G\text{,}\) consider the subgroup \(gPg\inv\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.94. Problem 4.

Fix a prime number \(p\text{,}\) and let \(A\) denote the abelian group of all complex roots of unity whose orders are powers of \(p\text{;}\) that is
\begin{equation*} A = \{z \in \C | z^{p^n} = 1 \text{ for some integer } n \leq 1\} . \end{equation*}
Prove the following statements. (a) Every non-trivial subgroup of \(A\) contains the group of \(p^{\text{th}}\) roots of unity. (b) Every proper subgroup of \(A\) is cyclic. (c) If \(B\) and \(C\) are subgroups of \(A\text{,}\) then either \(B \subseteq C\) or \(C \subseteq B\text{.}\) (d) For each \(n \geq 0\) there exists a unique subgroup of \(A\) with \(p^n\) elements.
Solution.

Problem F.95. Problem 5.

Let F be a field, and let \(f (x) ∈ F [x]\text{.}\) Recall that \(f (x)\) is separable provided, for every extension field \(K/F , f (x)\) has no multiple roots in \(K\text{.}\) (A multiple root is an element \(α ∈ K\) such that \((x − α)^2 | f (x)\) in \(K[x].)\)
  1. Prove that \(f (x) ∈ F [x]\) is separable if and only if \(f (x)\) and its derivative \(f'(x)\) are relatively prime in \(F [x]\text{.}\)
  2. Suppose that \(f (x)\) is irreducible and that the degree of \(f (x)\) is not a multiple of the characteristic of \(F\text{.}\) Prove that \(f (x)\) is separable.
Solution.

Problem F.96. Problem 6.

Let \(E\) be the splitting field of the polynomial \(x^7 − 12\) over \(\Q\text{.}\) Find \([E : \Q]\text{,}\) and describe the elements of \(\Gal(E/\Q)\) explicitly.
Solution.

Problem F.97. Problem 7.

Let p be a prime number, let \(\F_p\) be the field with \(p\) elements, and let \(n\) be a positive integer. Prove that \(x^{p^n}- x =\prod_{d|n} g_d(x)\text{,}\) where, for a positive divisor \(d\) of \(n\text{,}\) \(g_d(x)\) denotes the product of all monic irreducible polynomials of degree \(d\) in \(\F_p[x]\text{.}\) (You may assume basic results on the structure of finite fields and their subfields.)
Solution.

Problem F.98. Problem 8.

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring with \(1\text{.}\) Recall that \(r \in R\) is called [[Mathematics/Definitions/Nilpotent|nilpotent]] i/f \(r^n = 0\) for some integer \(n > 0.\) Let \(N\) be the set of nilpotent elements of \(R\text{.}\) (a) Show that \(N\) is an ideal of \(R\text{.}\) (b) Show that the ring \(R/N\) has no nonzero nilpotent elements. (c) For a polynomial \(f(x) \in R[x]\text{,}\) prove that \(f(x)\) is a nilpotent element of \(R[x]\) if and only if every coefficient of \(f(x)\) is nilpotent.
Solution.

Problem F.99. Problem 9.

Consider the following matrix over \(\Q\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix}-1&0&0&0\\0&-1&0&0\\-2&-2&0&1\\-2&0&-1&-2\end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
(a) Show that the characteristic and minimal polynomials of \(A\) are, respectively, \((x + 1)^4\) and \((x + 1)^3\text{.}\) (b) Find the rational canonical form \(R\) of \(A\) and the Jordan canonical form \(J\) of \(A\text{.}\) (c) Find an invertible matrix \(P\) such that \(P\inv AP = J\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.100. Problem 10.

Let \(A\) and \(B\) be \(n\times n\) matrices with entries in \(F\text{.}\) Recall that \(A\) and \(B\) are said to be similar over \(F\) if there exists an [[Mathematics/Definitions/Invertible Matrix|invertible]] \(n\times n\) matrix, with entries in \(F\text{,}\) such that \(B = P\inv AP\text{.}\) Prove the following statements about matrices \(A\) and \(B\) with entries in \(F\text{:}\)
  1. If \(F \subseteq K\) is a field extension, and \(A\) and \(B\) are similar over \(K\text{,}\) then they are similar over \(F\text{.}\)
  2. \(A\) is similar over \(F\) to its [[Mathematics/Definitions/Transpose|transpose]] \(A^T\text{.}\)
Solution.
