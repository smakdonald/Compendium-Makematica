\(\rem\).
The previous proof used the following fact implicitly:
If \(W\) is a subspace of a finite dimensional \(F\)-vector space \(V\) (where \(F\) is a field) and \(\dim_F(W) = \dim_F(V)\text{,}\) then \(W = V\text{.}\)
This holds since any basis of \(W\) can be extended to a basis of \(V\) and hence if \(W\) is properly contained in \(V\) we must have \(\dim_F(W) < \dim_F(V)\text{.}\)
This fact fails for commutative rings in general. For instance, let \(V = \Z\) and \(W = \Z \cdot 2\text{.}\) Then \(V\) and \(W\) are free of rank one and \(W\) is a submodule of \(V\text{,}\) but \(W \ne V\text{.}\)
