Section I: Group Theory

Problem F.129. Problem 1.

Let \(G\) be a (not necessarily finite) group and \(K \leq G\) a subgroup of index \(n < \infty\text{.}\) Define
\begin{equation*} N=\bigcap_{g\in G}gKg\inv \end{equation*}
(i.e., \(N\) is the intersection of all the conjugates of K\()\)
  1. Prove \(N\) is a the largest normal subgroup of \(G\) that is contained in \(K\text{.}\)
  2. Prove \([G : N ]\) divides \(n!\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.132. Problem 4.

Let \(L\) be the splitting field over \(\Q\) of the polynomial \(x^3-7\text{.}\) (a) Find all intermediate fields \(E\) with \(\Q \sse E \sse L\) (including possibly \(L\) and \(\Q\)) such that \(E\) is Galois over \(\Q\text{.}\) (b) For each field \(E\) you found in (a), find with justification a primitive generator (i.e., find \(\a \in E\) so that \(E = \Q(\a))\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.133. Problem 5.

Let \(p\) be any positive prime integer.
  1. Prove that if \(p = k^2 + 1\) for some integer k, then p is not an irreducible element of \(\Z[i]\text{.}\)
  2. Prove \(x^4-p\) is irreducible in \(\Q(i)[x]\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.134. Problem 6.

For fields \(E\) and \(F\text{,}\) we say \(E\) is a finite splitting field over \(F\) if \(E\) is the splitting field over \(F\) of some polynomial \(f(x)\in F [x]\text{.}\) Assume \(E\) and \(K\) are both finite splitting fields over \(\Q\) and prove \(E \cap K\) is also a finite splitting field over \(\Q\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.135. Problem 7.

Let \(F\) be a field and recall that a square matrix \(A\) with entries in \(F\) is called [[Mathematics/Definitions/Nilpotent|nilpotent]] if \(A^m = 0\) for some positive integer \(m\text{.}\)
  1. Prove that if \(A\) is an \(n \times n\) nilpotent matrix, then \(A^n = 0\text{.}\)
  2. Assume \(n \leq 3\) and prove that two \(n \times n\) nilpotent matrices are similar if and only if they have the same [[Mathematics/Definitions/Rank|rank]]. (Recall the rank of a matrix is the [[Mathematics/Definitions/Dimension (Vector Space)|dimension]] of the vector space spanned by its columns.)
  3. Give an example, with justification, of two \(4 \times 4\) nilpotent matrices that have the same rank but are not similar.
Solution.
