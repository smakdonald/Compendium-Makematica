Section I: Group Theory

Problem F.175. Problem 1.

Let \(G\) be a finite \(p\)-group for some prime \(p\) and \(N\neq {1}\) a normal subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) Prove that \(N\cap Z(G)\neq{1}\text{,}\) where \(Z(G)\) is the center of \(G\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.176. Problem 2.

A non-abelian group of order \(27\text{.}\) (a) Prove there exists a non-abelian group of \(27\text{.}\) (Hint: Use a semi-direct product.) (b) Find, with justification, a presentation of the group you found in part (a).
Solution.

Problem F.177. Problem 3.

Let G be a group of order \(2^5\cdot 7^3\text{.}\) Prove that \(G\) is not simple.
Solution.
Section II:

Problem F.178. Problem 4.

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring with identity, and assume \(1\neq0\text{.}\) Let \(I\) and \(J\) be ideals such that \(I + J = R\text{.}\)
  1. Prove \(IJ = I\cap J\text{.}\)
  2. Prove the following special case of the Sunzi's Remainder Theorem: There is an isomorphism of rings of the form \(R/(I \cap J)\cong R/I \times R/J\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.179. Problem 5.

Prove the Rank-Nullity Theorem: If \(F\) is a field, \(V\) and \(W\) are a finite dimensional \(F\) -vector spaces, and \(g : V \to W\) is an \(F\) -linear transformation, then
\begin{equation*} \rank(g) + \nullity(g) = \dim(V). \end{equation*}
(Recall the rank of \(g\) is the dimension of its image and the nullity of \(g\) is the dimension of its kernel.)
Solution.

Problem F.180. Problem 6.

Let \(F\) be a field and \(n\) a positive integer. We say an \(n\times n\) matrix \(A\) with entries in \(F\) is unipotent if \(A-In\) is [[Mathematics/Definitions/Nilpotent|nilpotent]] (i.e., \((A-In)^k = 0\) for some \(k \geq 1\)). For the field \(F = \Q\text{,}\) find (with complete justification) the number of similarity classes of \(4\times 4\) unipotent matrices and give an explicit representative for each class.
Solution.

Problem F.181. Problem 7.

Let \(L\) be the splitting field of \(x^5 - 11\) over \(\Q\)
  1. Find, with justification, the degree of \(L\) over \(\Q\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(F = \Q(\zeta)\) where \(\zeta = e^{2\pi i/5}\text{,}\) a primitive \(5\th\) root of unity. Prove \(x^5 - 11\) is irreducible in \(F[x]\text{.}\)
Solution.

Problem F.182. Problem 8.

Let \(L\) be the splitting field of \(x^4-2022\) over \(\Q\text{.}\) Prove there exists a unique [[Mathematics/Definitions/Intermediate Field|intermediate]] field \(\Q\subseteq K\subseteq L\) such that \([K : \Q] = 4\) and \(\Q\subseteq K\) is a Galois extension.
Solution.

Problem F.183. Problem 9.

Assume \(F\subseteq L\) is an algebraic field extension such that every non-constant polynomial in \(F[x]\) splits completely into linear factors in \(L[x]\text{.}\) Prove \(L\) is an [[Mathematics/Definitions/Algebraic Closure|algebraic closure]] of \(F\text{.}\)
Solution.
